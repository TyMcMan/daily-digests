High-speed non-volatile barium titanate field-programmable photonic gate array

Nature Photonics reports a programmable photonic array built from BaTiO3 phase shifters in a hexagonal waveguide mesh, with non-volatile state retention and sub-100-nanosecond reconfiguration. Why it matters: a fast, non-volatile, mesh-based FPPGA is a core building block for reconfigurable optical compute and signal processing — directly relevant to programmable PIC architectures.

Orbital angular momentum multiplexing diffractive neural networks for high-capacity optical inference

A deep diffractive neural network uses OAM multiplexing to perform multitask recognition across 100 object categories at 72.7% average accuracy, raising the channel capacity of free-space optical inference. Why it matters: OAM multiplexing is a path to scaling the throughput of passive optical neural networks without added electronic overhead.

Rapid Gaussian Boson Sampling Circuit Screening for GKP State Creation via a Two-Stage Machine Learning Surrogate

The authors train a two-stage histogram gradient-boosting surrogate that predicts which Gaussian boson sampling circuits will yield GKP states, hitting 90% detection accuracy on held-out data and cutting screening cost by roughly 90%. Why it matters: GKP states are a key resource for fault-tolerant photonic quantum computing, and cheap circuit screening accelerates the all-photonic route to generating them.

EPFL builds photonic chip that rivals much larger laboratory lasers

EPFL compressed a 42-cm Mamyshev-oscillator laser cavity onto an erbium-doped silicon-nitride chip, delivering 1.05 nJ pulses as short as 147 fs from a matchhead-sized device. Why it matters: integrated ultrafast sources on a CMOS-compatible SiN platform are enabling building blocks for on-chip frequency combs, optical clocks and PIC-based metrology.

Electrically tuning second-harmonic generation

An Emory University team built a few-nanometer plasmonic tunnel junction (gold electrodes, epitaxial lutetium-oxide barrier) whose second-harmonic output can be electrically modulated by ~500%. Why it matters: compact, electrically tunable nonlinear light sources are candidate elements for integrated optical modulators and on-chip frequency conversion.
