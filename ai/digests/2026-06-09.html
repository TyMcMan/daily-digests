RREDCoT: Segment-Level Reward Redistribution for Reasoning Models

GRPO-based RL fine-tuning assigns reward only at the end of a full Chain-of-Thought trace, creating high-variance Monte Carlo training. RREDCoT adapts RUDDER-style reward redistribution to the CoT MDP, using the generating LM itself plus a Bayesian-inspired value estimator to assign credit to important trace segments — no auxiliary model needed. Evaluated on AIME, it raises pass rate from 85% to 90.8%.

Why it matters: A zero-overhead technique for making RL reasoning training more stable and data-efficient without adding inference cost.

MDP-GRPO: Stabilized Group Relative Policy Optimization for Multi-Constraint Instruction Following

Standard GRPO breaks down on discrete, low-dispersion rewards from multi-constraint tasks: whole groups get identical scores, gradients vanish. MDP-GRPO fixes this via (1) multi-temperature sampling to spread reward, (2) dual-anchor advantages for homogeneous groups, (3) prospect-theoretic shaping, and (4) asymmetric KL regularization. Outperforms GRPO on FollowBench and IFEval with better strict constraint satisfaction.

Why it matters: Makes GRPO practical for the increasingly common use-case of instruction-following with multiple simultaneous format and content constraints.

Policy-Conditioned Counterfactual Credit for Verifiable RL of Long-Horizon Language Agents

Long-horizon language agents trained with verifiable rewards still learn unsupported inference chains and shortcut actions that satisfy terminal checks. CVT-RL proposes a constrained policy-gradient algorithm with dense verifiable rewards, intervention-validity gating, and a policy-conditioned counterfactual contribution (PCCC) estimator that attributes credit correctly across long trajectories.

Why it matters: Directly attacks the credit-assignment problem for agents that must take many sequential tool calls before any verifiable reward signal arrives.

GRAIL: Gradient-Reweighted Advantages for Reinforcement Learning with Verifiable Rewards

GRPO with verifiable rewards is now a standard recipe for mathematical reasoning, but advantage estimates are noisy when groups of rollouts contain many correct or many incorrect answers. GRAIL reweights gradient contributions based on group composition, reducing variance and improving training efficiency on math benchmarks without architectural changes.

Why it matters: A simple GRPO improvement that can be dropped into existing RLVR pipelines for free gain.
