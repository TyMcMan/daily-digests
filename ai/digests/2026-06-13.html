Role-Agent: Bootstrapping LLM Agents via Dual-Role Evolution

A single LLM simultaneously plays the agent (Actor) and simulates the world (World-In-Agent). Predicted post-action states are compared to actual states; the gap drives a process reward signal. The co-evolution of agent policy and environment model improves both without external environment rollouts.

Why it matters: Removes dependency on expensive or unavailable external simulators for agent training; the dual-role self-play loop could bootstrap capable agents from minimal real interaction.
