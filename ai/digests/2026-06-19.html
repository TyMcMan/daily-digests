VibeThinker-3B: Exploring the Frontier of Verifiable Reasoning in Small Language Models

A 3.09B-parameter model fine-tuned from Qwen2.5-Coder-3B via diversity-driven RL achieves 80.2 on LCBv6 and 94.3 on AIME 2026, matching Opus 4.5-level coding performance. The post-training pipeline uses high-signal synthetic data with verifiable solutions, multi-path reasoning filtering, two-stage SFT, and checkpoint selection by target accuracy rather than validation loss.

Why it matters: Demonstrates frontier-tier coding at a ~$25–60k training budget, making strong post-training accessible beyond large labs.

Can LLM Agents Infer World Models? Evidence from Agentic Automata Learning

Uses deterministic finite automata (DFA) as a controlled testbed for measuring whether tool-calling LLM agents can uncover hidden environments via membership and equivalence queries. Performance drops sharply with DFA size; reasoning models outperform non-reasoning models, but all struggle with query planning, evidence integration, and hypothesis construction.

Why it matters: Exposes a structural gap in reasoning agents beyond surface-level task completion — they fail at systematic world-model inference.

Evaluates MLLMs capable of processing text and visual inputs on physics visual tasks, finding performance ranges from 81.5% to 21%. Expensive models do not reliably outperform cheaper alternatives, challenging the cost-capability assumption in multimodal deployments.

ProPlay: Procedural World Models for Self-Evolving LLM Agents

Introduces a procedural world model that lets agents rehearse future procedural paths via "preplay," represented as a procedure graph of successful task trajectories with reliability-weighted edges. Evaluated on τ-Bench, ScienceWorld, and PlanCraft; closes the loop between memory and planning that prior agent methods left open.

Why it matters: Self-evolving agents that update their world model from experience — without external supervision — are a prerequisite for robust long-horizon autonomy.
