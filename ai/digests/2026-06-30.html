Formalizing Latent Thoughts: Four Axioms of Thought Representation in LLMs

Proposes four axioms — Causality, Minimality, Separability, Stability — with benchmark-independent metrics for evaluating LLM reasoning representations. Auditing 23 reasoning tasks finds no existing representation satisfies all four; internal representations encode little beyond input information.
Why it matters: A principled, task-agnostic framework to pressure-test CoT interpretability claims. If "chain of thought" fails Causality or Separability, it may be pattern-matching, not reasoning — and this paper gives you the tools to check.

When Are Likely Answers Right? On Sequence Probability and Correctness in LLMs

Shows that higher sequence probability predicts correctness across prompt-answer pairs within a dataset, but this does not transfer to decoding decisions: increasing probability via hyperparameter changes (temperature, beam width) or sampling methods does not reliably improve accuracy.
Why it matters: Directly challenges standard decoding heuristics. If higher-probability decoding doesn't yield more correct answers, many common inference-time scaling intuitions need revisiting.

From Signals to Transfer: Probe-Based Uncertainty Estimation in Large Language Models

A factorized study of probe-based hallucination detection: raw hidden states outperform in-domain, but structured/compressed features are more robust under distribution shift, yielding transferable off-the-shelf probes for open-ended factual generation without task-specific calibration.
Why it matters: Transferable hallucination probes are drop-in uncertainty signals across deployments — practical reliability tooling that works without per-task training overhead.

Ko-WideSearch: A Korean Breadth-Search Benchmark for Web Agents

Targets breadth-search (exhaustive set enumeration with per-item attribute tables) rather than depth-search in Korean web agent tasks. Spans 228 tables over 190 entities across 16 categories at three difficulty tiers — addressing a significant gap in web agent evaluation for non-English breadth retrieval.
Why it matters: Web agent benchmarks have been almost exclusively English and depth-search. Real enterprise web agents need breadth retrieval with attribute extraction across languages — this benchmark makes that measurable.

PhysisForcing: Physics Reinforced World Simulator for Robotic Manipulation

Combines physical constraints with learned world models to improve policy robustness and sim-to-real transfer for robotic manipulation. Physics-reinforced simulation anchors learned dynamics to known physical laws, reducing the distributional gap that plagues learned world models.
Why it matters: Sim-to-real gap is the primary barrier to reliable robot deployment. Physics-anchored world models are a direct path to more trustworthy policy transfer without additional real-world data collection.
