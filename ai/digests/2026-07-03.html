SuperCorrect: Recalibrating LLM Overconfidence in Self-Correction

Investigates why LLMs frequently worsen their answers when asked to self-correct (the overconfidence problem) and introduces SuperCorrect, a supervised fine-tuning framework that teaches models when to trust versus revise initial outputs. Evaluated across math and code reasoning benchmarks with consistent accuracy gains.

Why it matters: Directly addresses a key reliability issue in agentic pipelines where models loop on correct answers — fixing this reduces unnecessary retries and improves multi-step agent performance.
