Introduces a unified benchmark with 42 diverse sim tasks spanning five capability dimensions (pick & place, articulated manipulation, dexterous grasping, long-horizon, multi-step) and 18 real-world tasks across three robot embodiments, backed by a public leaderboard. Accepted to ECCV 2026.

Agon: Competitive Cross-Model RL with Implicit Rival Grading of Reasoning

Makes two competing LLMs each other's implicit graders: both solve the same problem, one drafts a solution the other can see, and each is rewarded for out-solving the rival. Reasoning quality is judged via competitive pressure — no process labels, no reward model required.
Why it matters: Directly addresses GRPO/RLVR's failure mode of rewarding verbosity over thinking quality; offers a process-reward-free path to better reasoning without human-annotated reasoning traces.

The Key to Going Linear: Analysis-Driven Transformer Linearization

An analysis-driven framework for converting pretrained transformer LLMs into linear recurrent architectures with minimal fine-tuning cost, identifying which structural properties matter most for capability preservation during the conversion.
Why it matters: Reduces inference from O(n²) to O(n) for existing pretrained checkpoints without full retraining — direct serving-cost and latency impact for practitioners deploying large models at scale.

RLVP: Penalize the Path, Reward the Outcome

An RL training approach for LLMs that penalizes the reasoning trace while rewarding correct final outcomes, discouraging unnecessary verbosity in chain-of-thought without requiring process supervision labels. Complements RLVR methods with a lightweight path-level signal.
Why it matters: Tackles the over-thinking problem in reasoning models; practical for teams dealing with token-bloated CoT outputs where inference cost scales directly with reasoning trace length.
cs.LGRLVREfficiencyarxiv.org

Dual Latent Memory in Vision-Language-Action Models for Robotic Manipulation

A dual-memory architecture for VLA models fusing short-term working memory with long-term episodic and semantic memory, achieving 96.5% success in simulation and 84.0% real-world success on non-Markovian manipulation tasks where single-observation models fail.
Why it matters: Explicit memory separation (working vs. episodic/semantic) is a practical transferable pattern for any agent requiring temporally coherent action over long horizons; strong empirical gains validate the architecture for real deployments.
