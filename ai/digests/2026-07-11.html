SAO: Single-Rollout Asynchronous Optimization for Agentic RL

Tsinghua's SAO addresses the stability/off-policy problem in asynchronous RL for LLM post-training. It replaces group-wise sampling with single-rollout sampling and adds strict double-side token-level clipping. SAO trains stably for 1,000 steps and was used to train GLM-5.2 (750B-A40B).
Why it matters: Async RL is the direction for long-horizon agentic tasks; SAO makes it stable — and it's already powering a frontier open model.

OpenCoF: Learning to Reason Through Video Generation

Introduces a chain-of-thought-style framework where an LMM learns reasoning by generating intermediate video frames as "visual thoughts." Shows that generative video steps improve multimodal reasoning on downstream tasks beyond text-only chain-of-thought.

UP: Unbounded Positive Asymmetric Optimization for RL Post-Training

Formalises "Probability Capacity" (Cap) and shows conservative clipping in importance-sampling RL (GRPO et al.) structurally stifles exploration by truncating updates for correct but low-confidence reasoning paths. UP removes the upper clip asymmetrically, unlocking the exploration budget without destabilising training.
Why it matters: Better RL post-training recipes translate directly into smarter reasoning models — UP is a principled, drop-in improvement over GRPO.
