📄 Research & Labs

Single-Step Lithography for Reconfigurable SiN Photonics with TiN Heaters and Al Interconnects

Presents a single-mask fabrication process that co-defines TiN thermo-optic phase shifters, Al interconnects, and SiN waveguides in one lithography step on platforms at 810 nm and 1550 nm, achieving π-shift powers of ~92 mW and ~120 mW respectively. Eliminates separate patterning steps for heaters, wiring, and contact pads. Why it matters: Directly reduces the process complexity and cost of reconfigurable SiN PIC manufacturing — enabling lower-cost thermo-optically controlled photonic compute fabrics at foundry scale.

Perturbative Photonic Matrix-Vector Multiplication with Reduced Phase-Shift Rangecatch-up

Introduces a perturbative programming method for MZI-based photonic matrix-vector multiply (MVM) circuits that operates near a fixed reference configuration and uses interferometric subtraction to achieve the target linear transform with dramatically reduced phase excursion per modulator — applicable to both universal unitary meshes and low-depth non-unitary constructions. Why it matters: Reducing required phase-shift range relaxes modulator specifications and lowers drive-power requirements, a direct practical benefit for scaling photonic tensor cores in optical inference accelerators.

Reconfigurable Single-Ring Photonic Molecule on Thin-Film Lithium Niobatecatch-up

Demonstrates a photorefractive grating written by optical interference inside a single TFLN racetrack resonator that hybridises co-propagating transverse modes, forming a "photonic molecule" without needing two coupled rings. The grating is optically programmable, erasable, and rewritable — providing non-volatile frequency spectrum control without continuous electrical bias. Why it matters: Optically programmable resonators with no continuous power draw are a key component for low-energy WDM channel management in photonic compute interconnects.

Broadband Chromatic Dispersion of Thermo-Refractive Coefficients in Silicon Nitride Nonlinear Photonicscatch-up

Characterises the wavelength-dependent thermo-refractive coefficient of SiN — the dominant tuning handle in integrated nonlinear PIC processes (frequency conversion, soliton microcombs). Shows this dispersion significantly affects phase-matching predictions and must be accounted for in thermal tuning models. Why it matters: Impacts design accuracy for SiN chips that use temperature to tune frequency converters or optical clocks feeding photonic compute engines; incorrect thermal models lead to systematic phase-mismatch errors.

Photonics21 Hands "Light Driving the Future" SRIA to European Commission in Brussels

At the Photonics Partnership Annual Meeting in Brussels (June 9–10), Photonics21 formally delivered its updated Strategic Research and Innovation Agenda to the European Commission. The SRIA warns that Europe cannot take its photonics lead for granted — China's market share rose from 10% (2005) to 32% (2022) while Europe's slipped to 15% — and calls for a €2 billion standalone EU program in the 2028–2034 FP10 budget cycle covering AI, semiconductor manufacturing, healthcare, communications, and quantum. Why it matters: This is the EU photonics sector's most significant policy statement ahead of FP10; its reception determines whether integrated photonics gets a dedicated budget line in the next Framework Programme — directly shaping the funding landscape for Akhetonics.

EU Chips Act 2.0 Explicitly Lists Photonic Integrated Circuits as Key Semiconductor Technology

The European Commission's Chips Act 2.0 proposal (tabled June 3; industry response published June 8–10) explicitly includes photonic integrated circuit technologies as a key enabling semiconductor, creating a dedicated PIC pillar covering design tools, libraries, pilot lines, and demonstrators under the Chips for Europe Initiative 2.0. European PIC ecosystem players have been actively lobbying for this inclusion. Why it matters: Creates the legal basis for dedicated EU funding of InP and SiN foundry infrastructure in the next industrial policy cycle — a policy win years in the making for European PIC companies including Akhetonics.
