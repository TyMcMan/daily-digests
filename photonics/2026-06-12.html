Ayar Labs Joins NVIDIA NVLink Fusion Ecosystem with TeraPHY Co-Packaged Optics

Ayar Labs' TeraPHY™ in-package optical I/O chiplet is now optically and electrically qualified as part of NVIDIA's NVLink Fusion open-ecosystem program, enabling system builders to construct fully optically connected AI racks around NVIDIA's platform. The announcement follows Ayar's $500 M Series E ($3.75 B valuation) and a rack-scale CPO reference design shown at OFC 2026 with Wiwynn. Why it matters: Two major CPO vendors (Lightmatter and Ayar) are now co-certified in NVIDIA's ecosystem within days of each other, cementing 2027–2028 as the CPO production ramp window and creating a de-facto standard interconnect stack for AI datacenter builders.

TrendForce: Combined EML + CW-DFB Laser Monthly Capacity Hits 50.7 M Units in 2026

Suppliers have doubled combined EML and CW-DFB laser diode monthly production capacity to ~50.7 M units in 2026, driven by hyperscalers (NVIDIA, Google, Meta) locking in multi-year supply. Broadcom, Lumentum, and Sumitomo Electric hold ~55% of market capacity; Lumentum demonstrated 400 Gb/s-per-lane EML at OFC 2026 targeting 3.2 Tb/s module architectures. Why it matters: The laser supply chain is being restructured at scale for CPO — but NVIDIA strategic lock-in means late entrants to CPO face potential sourcing bottlenecks through 2027. Vertical integration of the light source is increasingly a competitive moat.

QST Japan: Light-Driven Magnetic Memory Switches 1,000× Faster Than Electrical Methods

A team led by Japan's National Institutes for Quantum Science and Technology (QST) has published in Applied Physics Letters a new magnetic memory material where laser light reverses magnetic states roughly 1,000 times faster than conventional electrically driven switching while substantially reducing heat and energy loss. The researchers note it may eventually serve as a photoelectric conversion interface linking optical interconnects to electronic circuits on the same chip platform. Why it matters: For photonic compute architects: this points toward on-chip optical memory elements that match optical interconnect speeds, removing one of the key bottlenecks at the photonic–electronic boundary.

📄 Research & Labs

Monash University: First Fully Integrated Valleytronic System on a Chip

Researchers at Monash University and international collaborators have demonstrated the first on-chip system that generates, steers, and reads light-based information using the "valley" quantum degree of freedom of atomically thin materials integrated with nanoscale photonic structures — all on a single device. The system operates at room temperature and was published in Nature Photonics. Why it matters: Valleytronics encodes information in a quantum property beyond standard polarization or intensity, opening a potential new degree of freedom for photonic logic. Room-temperature operation is the key enabling milestone for practical integration with conventional PIC stacks.

Yaqumo, NKT Photonics & Hamamatsu Photonics Form Quantum Photonics Alliance

European quantum hardware startup Yaqumo has signed an alliance agreement with NKT Photonics (Denmark, fiber lasers) and Hamamatsu Photonics (Japan, photodetectors) to industrialize cold-atom quantum core components. The collaboration targets standardized, multi-functional integrated optical engine modules combining NKT's high-stability fiber laser technology with Hamamatsu's detection capabilities and Yaqumo's hardware-software co-design. Why it matters: Signals convergence between EU photonics component manufacturers and quantum-hardware startups on shared module standards — the same integration pattern (laser + detector + PIC) relevant to photonic compute.
