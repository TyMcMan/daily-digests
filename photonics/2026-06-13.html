Penn Physicists Achieve All-Optical Nonlinear Switching via Exciton-Polaritons at 4 Attojoulescatch-up

University of Pennsylvania researchers demonstrated all-optical logic switching using exciton-polaritons — hybrid light–matter quasiparticles formed by strongly coupling photons to electrons in an atomically thin 2D semiconductor — consuming just ~4 attojoules per switching event. Because photonic AI chips typically must convert optical signals back to electrical at every nonlinear activation step (incurring latency and energy overhead), this approach enables in-situ nonlinear optical computation without electrical conversion. Why it matters: Eliminating the photonic–electronic roundtrip at activation functions is the most-cited bottleneck for all-optical AI inference; polariton-based switching at attojoule energies is a credible path around it, though the 2D-material integration challenge with CMOS-compatible PICs remains open.

China’s Prinano Claims 8-Inch Photonic Chip Mass Production via Nanoimprint, Bypassing DUV

Hangzhou-based startup Prinano has validated mass production of photonic chips on 8-inch silicon wafers using its proprietary PL-AS vacuum air-cushion nanoimprint lithography (NIL) platform — physically pressing nanoscale patterns into resist rather than projecting light — in collaboration with Shenzhen Litra Technology. The company claims sub-10 nm line-width resolution and production costs roughly one-tenth of conventional DUV-based methods. No yield figures or customer shipment data were disclosed. Why it matters: If yield is substantive, NIL-based PIC production would decouple photonic chip manufacturing from scanner-supplier constraints (ASML), potentially disrupting the silicon photonics foundry cost structure for optical comms, LiDAR, and sensing PICs.

NUS: Self-Testing QRNG on Integrated Silicon Photonic Chip Certifies Randomness in Real Time

Researchers at the National University of Singapore fabricated a fully integrated silicon photonic quantum random number generator (QRNG) that self-tests its own hardware integrity using a measurement-device-independent (MDI) protocol, eliminating reliance on manufacturer certification. Both signal encoder and homodyne detector are co-integrated on a single 8-inch wafer silicon platform, producing 15.33 Mbits of certifiable randomness per run at room temperature. The work appeared in PRX Quantum. Why it matters: Device-independent security proofs have historically required bulky lab setups; this chip-scale MDI-QRNG opens a path to production-grade quantum security hardware from standard silicon photonics foundries — directly relevant to post-quantum secure interconnects and data-center key management.

Hamamatsu Develops World-Record 2.0 kW Continuous-Wave Laser Diode Bar

Hamamatsu Photonics announced the successful development of a 2.0 kW continuous-wave laser diode bar — claimed as a world record for a single bar — under a NEDO (New Energy and Industrial Technology Development Organization) Feasibility Study / Frontier Incubation program. Results are slated for presentation at the 30th International Semiconductor Laser Conference (ISLC 2026, Tampere, Finland, 14–18 June 2026). Target applications include high-power industrial laser processing and solid-state laser pumping. Why it matters: High-power, high-brightness diode bars directly set the ceiling for laser power available to next-generation co-packaged-optics transceivers and optical amplifiers; pushing the per-bar ceiling enables denser CPO module architectures without added laser count.

Waseda & Okayama Review Terahertz Biophotonics Path to Practical Biological Imaging

Associate Professor Kazunori Serita (Waseda University) and Special-Appointment Professor Masayoshi Tonouchi (Okayama University) published a comprehensive survey in the Journal of Physics Photonics on the state and roadmap of terahertz biophotonics — addressing the core limitations of low spatial resolution, water-induced absorption, slow imaging speed, and bulky instrumentation. The paper outlines interdisciplinary strategies combining THz near-field microscopy, AI-assisted signal processing, and integrated THz PIC elements to translate the technology from proof-of-concept to clinical and lab-on-chip applications. Why it matters: THz is increasingly relevant to photonic biosensing and on-chip spectroscopy; this survey provides a structured roadmap for the engineering barriers that PIC-based THz sensors must clear before realistic deployment.

SEALSQ Acquires Swiss Miraex SA — TFLT PIC Platform for Quantum Networking and LEO Satellite QKD

SEALSQ Corp completed the acquisition of Miraex SA (EPFL Innovation Park, Ecublens, Switzerland), which developed a proprietary Thin Film Lithium Tantalate (TFLT) Photonic Integrated Circuit platform enabling microwave-to-optical quantum transduction — converting quantum states of microwave photons onto optical-frequency photons while preserving phase entanglement. The TFLT PIC closes the quantum interconnect layer in SEALSQ’s Quantum Sovereign Vertical Stack and is targeted at its Quantum Orbital Space Cloud (QOSC) program deploying quantum-secure communications on WISeSat.Space Low Earth Orbit nanosatellites for defense, finance, and national security. Why it matters: Lithium tantalate PICs offer higher electro-optic coefficients and lower optical loss than TFLN (lithium niobate) for certain quantum transduction regimes; the acquisition signals growing commercial appetite for III–V-alternative PIC platforms beyond silicon photonics for quantum networking applications.
