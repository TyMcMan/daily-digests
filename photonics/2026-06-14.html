Flexcompute Pioneers Agent-Driven Loop for Autonomous Photonic Chip Designcatch-up

Flexcompute launched the first fully autonomous agentic design loop for photonic integrated circuits: AI agents propose geometries, invoke Tidy3D FDTD multiphysics simulations, check fabrication constraints against PDKs for 8+ foundries via PhotonForge, and iterate to tapeout-ready layouts — all without human intervention. A Flexagent MCP plugin enables Python-driven control of the loop. Why it matters: the first production-grade closed-loop AI-in-the-loop photonic simulation workflow tied to real foundry PDKs; it compresses weeks of manual design iteration into hours, directly relevant to any team running PIC design on silicon photonics or InP platforms.

gdsfactory v9.44.0 Released — Port Orientation Fixes and Dependency Updates

The open-source PIC layout and EPDA library released v9.44.0 on June 12, incorporating bug fixes for port orientation at component boundaries, dependency updates across ~16 merged PRs, and documentation improvements. The library now supports 42+ PDKs with integrated Ansys, Lumerical, Tidy3D, and MEEP simulation backends, covering GDS, OASIS, STL, and GERBER outputs with built-in DRC and LVS. Why it matters: staying current with gdsfactory releases ensures compatibility with the latest PDK libraries — including SMART Photonics InP and silicon photonics foundry updates — and with simulation-backend integrations that are actively evolving alongside agentic design tools.

EU Chips Act 2.0 Proposed — Photonics Industry Lobbies for Dedicated PIC Trackcatch-up

The European Commission proposed Chips Act 2.0 with strengthened supply-chain sovereignty measures in June 2026; simultaneously, the photonics industry is lobbying for a dedicated Photonic Integrated Circuit track within the Act, noting the original Chips Act failed to classify PICs as strategic semiconductors. The case rests on the three EU photonics pilot lines already in place: PIXEurope (€380M), APECS (€726M), and STARLight (€102M). Why it matters: a successful PIC track in Chips Act 2.0 would unlock direct state aid and pilot-line access for volume manufacturing scale-up — for an optical compute startup in the EU, this is the most consequential near-term policy development for accessing subsidised manufacturing capacity.

SMART Photonics Advances InP PIC Scaling Roadmap Toward 6-Inch Pilot Linecatch-up

SMART Photonics published its roadmap toward large-scale InP PIC manufacturing, emphasising that InP remains the only photonic platform capable of monolithically integrating light generation, modulation, detection, and transmission on a single chip. The 6-inch wafer pilot line at High Tech Campus Eindhoven (PIXEurope, in collaboration with TNO) is targeted for ~2027–28, projecting ~10,000 wafers/year to support millions of chips annually. 50+ validated PDK building blocks developed over a decade of manufacturing are included. Why it matters: accelerating availability of production-grade InP foundry capacity in Europe is critical for optical compute startups pursuing coherent on-chip laser integration strategies that silicon photonics cannot deliver without hybrid bonding.
