⚡ Photonic & Optical Compute

Oriole Networks + AMD Deploy World's First Commercial Pure-Photonic AI Network

UK startup Oriole Networks deployed its PRISM all-optical circuit-switching fabric at AMD's ARIA Scaling Inference Lab — no electronic switches in the data path. The system achieved an 81% reduction in core switch power and near-zero GPU idle time via nanosecond-latency optical circuit switching. The xPU-agnostic design is slated for broader industry rollout in 2027. Why it matters: First commercial-scale proof point that pure photonic switching can land in production at a major AI infrastructure facility — the power and latency numbers set the competitive benchmark for PIC-based compute interconnects.

📄 Research & Labs

Scalable Optical Neural Network via Nonlocally Coupled Coherent Photonic Processor

This paper addresses the quadratic scaling bottleneck of MZI-mesh ONNs by exploiting diffractive nonlocal coupling between optical modes on-chip. Cascaded multiport directional couplers (MDCs) interleaved with phase-shifter arrays implement a unitary converter requiring only 3N phase shifters for an N-dimensional unitary matrix — a dramatic reduction from the O(N²) component count of standard MZI meshes. Demonstrated on a silicon photonic chip. Why it matters: The 3N scaling law is a fundamental architectural improvement for ONN accelerator design that could make large-scale photonic MAC units practically feasible at dimensions previously limited by component count and insertion loss.

South Korean researchers demonstrated a photonic computing device that replaces electrons with photons for data processing, claiming a 100× speed increase over conventional silicon with drastically reduced thermal output. Photonics21 Annual Meeting: New EU SRIA Handed to European Commission

The Photonics Partnership Annual Meeting in Brussels (June 9–10) formally delivered the new Strategic Research and Innovation Agenda (SRIA) to the European Commission, with a stark warning that Europe's photonics leadership is at risk from global competition. Key priorities: AI energy demands, non-EU semiconductor supply chain dependence, quantum scaling, and secure communications. The Photonics21 Innovation Award went to ARQ Quantum Technologies (Barcelona) for quantum networking over fibre. Why it matters: The SRIA sets the direction for EU Horizon funding and Chips Act 2.0 photonics provisions — directly relevant for Akhetonics' EU funding and grant strategy over the next research cycle.

QuiX Quantum Joins QuantumBW + Photonics BW — NL–DE Photonic Quantum Corridor

QuiX Quantum formalized its presence in Baden-Württemberg by joining QuantumBW and Photonics BW, linking the Dutch InP integrated photonics supply chain (Twente/Eindhoven) with German high-tech manufacturing. GlobalFoundries SCALE: Industry's First OCI MSA-Compliant CPO Platform

GlobalFoundries launched SCALE (Silicon Photonics Co-packaged Advanced Light Engine), the first CPO platform explicitly compliant with the Optical Compute Interconnect (OCI) Multi-Source Agreement. It supports both CWDM and DWDM (8λ and 16λ bi-directional demonstrated natively), targeting AI scale-up fabrics. GF expects CPO revenue to double in 2026 and exceed $1B by 2028. Why it matters: OCI MSA compliance means GF's platform is interoperable with a multi-vendor ecosystem — any PIC designed to OCI specs can leverage GF's fab, directly shaping PDK choices for teams targeting interoperable CPO deployments.

ORCA Computing Deploys Room-Temperature Photonic Quantum System in London Data Center

ORCA Computing installed its PT Series room-temperature photonic quantum computers (Time-Bin Interferometer architecture, no dilution refrigerators required) in Digital Realty's London Innovation Lab, enabling enterprises to run hybrid quantum-classical workloads alongside standard GPU racks without infrastructure modification. This is the first deployment of a photonic quantum system inside a tier-1 colocation facility. Why it matters: Room-temperature photonic quantum operation in a standard data center rack is a direct proof point for PIC platform portability; ORCA's TBI approach is architecturally adjacent to linear optical quantum computing paths that PIC startups working on quantum photonics may encounter.
