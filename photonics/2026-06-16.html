🔧 PIC Design & EDA

kfactory 1.2.4 / 1.2.5 Released — Bug Fixes for KLayout-Backed PIC Layout

kfactory is the KLayout-backed cell-library engine powering gdsfactory’s photonic chip layout pipeline. Versions 1.2.4 and 1.2.5 shipped back-to-back (merged via gdsfactory PRs #3737 and #3743) to resolve geometry-snapping and layer-handling bugs surfaced by users; gdsfactory updated its minimum dependency to >=1.2.4 and is now tracking toward kfactory 1.3 (PR #3750). Why it matters: If you use gdsfactory for PIC layout, upgrading to kfactory >=1.2.5 before your next tapeout run is straightforward and closes a class of silent geometry errors that can go undetected until DRC.

📄 Research & Labs

$10 ConvSpec Brings Lab-Grade Spectroscopy to Wearables — Cambridge, Nature Photonics

University of Cambridge researchers demonstrated the ConvSpec (Convolutional Spectrometer), which cascades micro-ring resonators with periodic spectral responses to execute the convolution theorem on an input spectrum. Proportional tuning of the rings linearly shifts the spectral response; recovery uses fast Fourier transforms — overcoming the bandwidth/resolution/accuracy tradeoff that limits dispersive and FT miniaturized spectrometers. Fabricated cost is ~$10. Demonstrated across near-IR; suited for industrial quality control and real-time wearable health monitoring. Why it matters: Ring-resonator-based spectral engines are a natural PIC architecture target. The ConvSpec approach could be ported to a SiPh foundry process, extending PIC-based sensing into cost-sensitive portable markets where existing spectrometers are too bulky or expensive.

UC Berkeley Laser Phase Plate Unlocks Cryo-EM of Small Proteins — Published in Science

Holger Mueller’s group at UC Berkeley demonstrated a laser phase plate (LPP) for cryo-electron microscopy, coupling an intense continuous-wave laser into a high-finesse near-concentric Fabry–Pérot cavity co-aligned with the electron column. The intracavity laser shifts the phase of the electron beam, boosting contrast for small molecules below ~60 kDa — a regime previously inaccessible to conventional cryo-EM. The team resolved hemoglobin (64 kDa) and targets ~17 kDa with a focused-beam upgrade. Published in Science June 11. Why it matters: The LPP is a precision photonic instrument at its core: a high-power intracavity CW laser, ultra-stable cavity alignment, and phase-coherent beam engineering. The engineering challenges are directly adjacent to integrated photonics — cavity finesse, insertion loss, and mode stability translate to PIC resonator design intuitions.

💰 Funding & Markets

Phosio Raises $4M Seed — Thin-Film Oxide Waveguides for AR Smartglasses

Phosio (Portland, OR), co-founded by materials scientist Douglas Keszler, closed a $4M seed round led by MESH with participation from Sierra Ventures, TEL Venture Capital, Silicon Catalyst Ventures, and Willamette Valley Capital. The company develops high-refractive-index metal oxide thin films as optical waveguide core and cladding materials for lightweight AR eyewear, targeting the display/coupling layer in smartglasses where current planar waveguide combiners suffer coupling-efficiency losses. Funding accelerates commercialization and foundry partnerships. Why it matters: Waveguide material selection is a core constraint in integrated PIC design; high-index oxide platforms adjacent to SiN and SiPh are worth tracking as they mature toward foundry-compatible deposition processes.

PsiQuantum Opens Construct to Public — Fault-Tolerant Quantum Algorithm Design, Free Access

PsiQuantum’s Construct — initially gated to select academic and industry partners at launch (September 2025) — is now publicly accessible at no cost. The platform includes Circuit Designer (billion-gate logical circuit compilation), Workbench, and Resource Analyzer, purpose-built for fault-tolerant quantum computing (FTQC) at utility scale. In March 2026 it gained NVIDIA CUDA-Q integration delivering up to 450× GPU-accelerated state-vector simulation vs. CPU. Hardware-decoupled: compiled logical code arrays target any FTQC modality (photonic, superconducting, neutral-atom). Why it matters: PsiQuantum’s roadmap is photonic qubits fabricated via silicon photonics foundry processes; free access to their FTQC algorithm toolchain lets photonics engineers map circuit-level resource requirements onto physical PIC hardware targets ahead of hardware availability — a useful planning tool for anyone tracking the photonic quantum computing supply chain.
