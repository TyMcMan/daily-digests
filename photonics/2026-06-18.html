⚡ Photonic & Optical Compute

3D Programmable Photonic Neural Network via Reconfigurable Free-Space Diffractive Layerscatch-up

Researchers demonstrate a fully programmable 3D optical neural network built from stacked reconfigurable diffractive layers, achieving classification accuracy on par with digital counterparts at optical inference speeds. The architecture uses phase-only spatial light modulators as programmable layers, enabling in-situ gradient-free training via optical forward passes. Why it matters: Moving beyond fixed diffractive optical networks to fully reconfigurable 3D stacks is the key step toward general-purpose optical inference accelerators — the result is a direct proof point for the 3D free-space approach (cf. Lumai’s Series A) and benchmarks the flexibility gap versus PIC-based optical compute.

Inverse-Designed Nanophotonic Neural Networks for On-Chip Inferencecatch-up

A Nature Communications paper reports inverse-designed silicon nanophotonic structures that implement neural network inference entirely in passive waveguide geometry — no active phase shifters required. The designs are generated by adjoint optimization and fabricated in standard 220 nm SOI, achieving multi-class classification with femtojoule-per-operation energy. Why it matters: Passive inference networks with no electronics or tunable elements could underpin ultra-low-power edge photonic compute; the SOI-compatible process means any SiPh foundry (GlobalFoundries, imec, SMART) can fabricate these — a benchmark for energy efficiency that PIC-based optical compute startups will need to address.

🔧 PIC Design & EDA

Keysight Acquires VPIphotonics — System-Level Photonic Simulation Joins Keysight EDA

Keysight Technologies announced the acquisition of VPIphotonics (Berlin), the developer of VPItransmissionMaker and VPIcomponentMaker — the dominant system- and component-level photonic simulation tools used in telecom, datacom, and PIC design. The combined portfolio now spans RF/microwave (Keysight ADS), photonic component simulation (Lumerical, acquired 2020), and end-to-end system-level link simulation (VPI). Terms undisclosed. Why it matters: VPIphotonics’ tools are the standard for coherent transceiver and PIC link-budget simulation in the EU telecom industry; combined with Lumerical and Keysight’s existing PDK partnerships (GlobalFoundries PDK announced June 3), Keysight is consolidating the full photonic EDA stack — relevant for any design flow using VPI tools for link-level co-simulation alongside component-level PIC design.

📄 Research & Labs

Ultra-Low-Loss Si₃N₄ Waveguides with Sub-0.1 dB/m Loss via Optimised LPCVD Annealingcatch-up

This arXiv preprint reports silicon nitride waveguides with propagation loss below 0.1 dB/m at 1550 nm, achieved through a refined LPCVD annealing protocol that minimises N–H absorption and surface roughness simultaneously. The process is demonstrated on 4-inch wafers with >95% yield across die, and the authors benchmark against state-of-the-art ultralow-loss Si₃N₄ platforms. Why it matters: Sub-0.1 dB/m Si₃N₄ loss is the threshold for on-chip delay lines, gyroscopes, and narrow-linewidth laser feedback cavities; a yield-compatible LPCVD route removes the barrier to foundry-scale production of ultralow-loss Si₃N₄ PICs relevant for LiDAR, sensing, and quantum photonics.

🇪🇺 EU Photonics & Manufacturing

Electro Optic Systems Establishes European C2/AI Hub in France with €10M Investment

Australian-listed Electro Optic Systems (EOS) announced a €10M investment to establish a European command-and-control and AI hub in France, targeting laser-based space situational awareness (SSA), directed energy, and optical communications markets. The French hub will integrate EOS’s free-space optical communications terminals and AI-driven tracking systems, leveraging France’s dual-use photonics ecosystem and EU defence procurement channels. Why it matters: EOS’s move signals growing defence and space demand for precision free-space optical terminals in Europe — a market vertical adjacent to the integrated PIC space-optical communications segment, and a signal of EU defence budget flow into photonics-enabled C2 systems post-2026 NATO commitments.

💰 Funding & Markets

Coherent Receives $50M CHIPS Act Letter of Intent for AI Laser & Optical Manufacturing

Coherent Corp. announced a Letter of Intent (LOI) from the US Commerce Department under the CHIPS and Science Act for up to $50M in direct funding to expand its manufacturing of high-power lasers and optical components for AI data-centre infrastructure. The investment targets Coherent’s vertical-cavity surface-emitting laser (VCSEL) and EML production lines, which supply optical transceivers and co-packaged optics modules for hyperscale AI clusters. Why it matters: CHIPS Act money flowing to laser/photonic component manufacturing — not just silicon fabs — confirms that optical interconnect supply chains are now viewed as strategic US infrastructure; the EML focus directly tightens the same InP substrate supply chain highlighted in yesterday’s China optical chip analysis.

IQE and Tower Semiconductor Sign Multi-Year InP Epiwafer Supply Agreement

IQE plc (Cardiff) and Tower Semiconductor announced a multi-year supply agreement under which IQE will provide InP epiwafers for Tower’s photonic foundry operations. The deal covers InP-based epitaxial wafers for Tower’s TPSCo (Japan) and Texas fabs, which serve datacom and telecom optical transceiver customers. IQE cited the agreement as a key step in securing European epiwafer supply chain capacity for the growing InP PIC market. Why it matters: With InP substrate shortfalls running at 70% for high-end supply (per the China optical chip analysis), a named multi-year epiwafer supply deal between the world’s largest III–V epiwafer supplier and a major photonic foundry is directly relevant supply-chain intelligence for any InP PIC design house or foundry — including SMART Photonics and European InP-on-silicon efforts.
