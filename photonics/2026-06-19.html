⚡ Photonic & Optical Compute

Ultrafast Chip Brings the Energy: EPFL SiN Femtosecond Laser on a PIC

EPFL researchers have demonstrated a silicon nitride photonic chip that generates femtosecond optical pulses with peak powers rivalling bulky laboratory laser systems. The chip integrates anomalous-dispersion SiN waveguides with on-chip nonlinear soliton compression. Why it matters: Ultrashort-pulse generation on a PIC unlocks on-chip time-domain sensing, nonlinear frequency conversion, and coherent LiDAR without external bulk optics — directly relevant to miniaturised photonic compute and sensing front-ends.

OVHcloud Expands Sovereign QaaS Platform via Quandela's Belenos Photonic System

OVHcloud has commercially launched access to Quandela's Belenos 12-qubit photonic quantum computer through its sovereign cloud platform, marking the first commercial deployment of a photonic quantum-computing-as-a-service system on European infrastructure. The Belenos system uses time-multiplexed single-photon qubits and operates at room temperature. Why it matters: Demonstrates photonic QC is production-ready for sovereign cloud; directly relevant to Akhetonics' mapping of the EU quantum-photonic stack.

PsiQuantum Advances Utility-Scale Quantum Computing Project in Australia

PsiQuantum has broken ground on its Moreton Bay Central site in Queensland — the planned home for what the company calls the first utility-scale fault-tolerant quantum computer, built on silicon photonic chips fabricated at GlobalFoundries. The announcement follows the opening of a dedicated Test & Validation Lab at Griffith University housing the Poseidon custom cryogenic system for parallel photonic chip characterisation. Why it matters: Silicon photonic chip production at volume scale for QC validates PIC manufacturability at leading-edge CMOS nodes.

Organic Thin Film Brings Second-Order Nonlinear Effects to Silicon Photonics

Researchers have deposited a poled organic thin film on a silicon-on-insulator waveguide to enable χ(2) second-harmonic generation — a nonlinear effect normally absent in centrosymmetric Si. The approach is CMOS-process-compatible and opens a route to on-chip electro-optic modulation and frequency doubling without lithium niobate transfer-printing. Why it matters: A manufacturable path to χ(2) on standard SiPho platforms could simplify modulator design and PIC-level frequency conversion for integrated optical compute.

Photonic Quantum Neural Fields for Physics-Informed Scientific Machine Learning arXiv 2606.18713

Proposes photonic quantum neural fields — trainable physical representations built from optical phase shifts, linear interference networks, and single-photon measurement — as a native photonic hardware substrate for physics-informed machine learning. Demonstrates the approach on partial differential equation solving tasks with improved parameter efficiency over classical alternatives. Why it matters: Maps ML workloads directly onto photonic circuit primitives, bridging PIC design targets and scientific AI inference applications.

arXiv · June 17, 2026

Certification of the Genuine Resolution of Photon-Number-Resolving Detectors arXiv 2606.14365

Introduces an operational framework and scalable protocol for certifying how many photons a PNR detector can genuinely distinguish in a single measurement, using only coherent state probes. Applied to a 28-pixel superconducting nanowire single-photon detector (SNSPD), certifying genuine four-outcome resolution with explicit efficiency requirements. Why it matters: Standardised PNR benchmarking removes a critical uncertainty in photonic quantum circuit characterisation, gate fidelity validation, and threshold-crossing checks.

PsiQuantum Opens Test & Validation Lab at Griffith University

PsiQuantum launched a dedicated Test & Validation Lab at Griffith University's Nathan Campus in Brisbane, housing the custom Poseidon cryogenic system — an ultra-high-cooling-capacity unit with parallel-slotted chambers designed to simultaneously cycle, chill, and characterise multiple independent silicon photonic chips. The facility serves as the central calibration hub for the Omega photonic chipset and integration subsystems. Why it matters: Signals the transition from photonic QC R&D to industrial-scale chip characterisation; a model for dedicated PIC test infrastructure at volume production readiness.
