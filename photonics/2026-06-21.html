⚡ Photonic & Optical Compute

Tower Semiconductor & Marvell Ship Over 5 Million Coherent Photonic ICs

Tower Semiconductor and Marvell announced a production milestone of 5 million coherent photonic ICs shipped for AI data center interconnect, using Tower's SiPh platform with non-silicon material integration, 3D electronics-optics integration, and V-groove optical packaging. The shipments target high-speed coherent optical links inside AI clusters.

Why it matters: Five million coherent PICs represents meaningful volume proof — coherent PICs are far harder to manufacture at yield than direct-detect, making this a genuine supply-chain signal that high-performance optical interconnect is entering production scale.

TrendForce: CPO/NPO Market to Exceed $39B by 2030 at 37% CAGR

TrendForce released a forecast that the combined co-packaged optics (CPO) and near-package optics (NPO) market will grow from ~$100M in 2025 to over $39B by 2030. Near-package optics (NPO) is identified as the preferred near-term transition path for hyperscalers. CPO will hold only ~0.5% penetration of AI optical transceiver modules in 2026 but is forecast at 35% by 2030.

Why it matters: The ramp is steep and back-weighted — NPO as the bridging technology means the supply chain for CPO components needs to be in place now for a 2027–2028 volume ramp. Directly relevant to Akhetonics' market timing and positioning.

📄 Research & Labs

Phase Modulation Design for Testable Photonic Systems and Co-Packaged Optics

Agnihotri, Kalla, and Blair (University of Utah) compare thermal vs. carrier-based phase modulation in Mach-Zehnder and microring modulators, covering extinction ratio, tuning efficiency, power draw, and modulation bandwidth, with a PAM-4 SiPh transmitter achieving 25 Gbps as a design target. Co-packaged optics and testability are explicit design constraints throughout.

Why it matters: Directly actionable for PIC modulator design in CPO contexts — provides a side-by-side comparison of the two dominant modulation approaches under the power and bandwidth constraints that matter for co-packaged AI optics.

arXiv 2606.19674 · June 18, 2026

Integrated Photonic Neural Network with On-Chip Backpropagation Training (Nature)

Ashtiani, Idjadi, and Kim (Nokia Bell Labs) demonstrate end-to-end on-chip gradient-descent backpropagation training on a single photonic chip, with all linear and nonlinear computations on-chip, achieving >90% accuracy on two nonlinear classification tasks without a digital computer in the training loop.

Why it matters: This is arguably the most significant photonic ML training result to date — eliminating the digital co-processor from training removes a fundamental architectural compromise for on-chip learning and moves fully optical neural network training from concept to demonstrated reality.

DxPTA: Design Space Exploration for Photonic Transformer Acceleratorscatch-up

Researchers from NYU Abu Dhabi propose DxPTA, a constraint-aware hardware/software co-design methodology for photonic transformer accelerators using coherent optical dataflow. Parameter significance analysis navigates the design space under area, power, and latency budgets, enabling application-level evaluation rather than device-level benchmarking in isolation.

Why it matters: Prior photonic accelerator papers proposed hardware without application-level constraints, making comparison to electronics unreliable. DxPTA provides the missing framework for evaluating photonic transformer accelerators against real system budgets.

Multi-Channel Optical Vision Model: Spatial Multiplexing as a Trainable Dimensioncatch-up

Ali Momeni et al. demonstrate that spatial multiplexing in optical neural networks functions not just for parallel throughput but as a trainable representational coordinate, enabling a hybrid optical-electronic vision-language model where the ONN provides visual tokens to a digital transformer decoder.

Why it matters: Directly relevant to Akhetonics: shows how optical spatial channels can do representational work — not just bandwidth work — in an ONN, opening a path to richer optical feature extraction in photonic inference engines.

🇪🇺 EU Photonics & Manufacturing

CORNERSTONE Survey: 59% of SiPh Firms Say Their Country Lacks Scale-Up Infrastructure

A CORNERSTONE Photonics Innovation Centre survey of 500 silicon photonics decision-makers across the UK, US, Netherlands, Germany, and Spain found 59% say their country lacks infrastructure to move from research to commercialization, with 66% citing manufacturing access as the primary barrier. 48% said they could reach commercial revenue 7–12 months earlier with 25% faster prototyping cycles.

Why it matters: The EU market is structurally underserved for pilot-line and rapid-iteration foundry access — a gap that benefits vertically integrated players and highlights the value of reducing prototype-to-production cycle time, directly relevant to Akhetonics' positioning.

CamGraPhIC Receives €211M EU/Italian State Aid for Graphene Photonic Transceiverscatch-up

The European Commission approved €211M in Italian state aid for CamGraPhIC, a Cambridge University spinout developing graphene-based photonic transceivers consuming 80% less energy than conventional data center transceivers. A pilot manufacturing facility is planned near Milan by 2028.

Why it matters: Graphene electro-optic modulators are a direct competitor/collaborator technology to SiPh ring modulators and LiNbO3 platforms. The €211M scale signals EU commitment to keeping photonic component manufacturing in Europe — worth tracking the modulator performance claims and fab readiness.

Folio Photonics Closes $8M Series A for Nanophotonic Archival Storage

Folio Photonics closed an $8M Series A co-led by Material Impact and the O.H.I.O. Fund to productize multi-layer nanophotonic archival storage media aimed at hyperscaler AI knowledge retention workloads. Why it matters: An emerging photonic data infrastructure niche: GPUs generate intelligence, Folio retains it.
