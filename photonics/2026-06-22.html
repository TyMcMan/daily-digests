Quantum Pulse Ventures QP2.0: 10× Qubit Fidelity via Composite Coupler Redesign

Israeli startup Quantum Pulse Ventures unveiled QP2.0 at a conference in Edinburgh, featuring a redesigned universal directional coupler using composite waveguides instead of uniform waveguides. The change reduces sensitivity to fabrication variation, delivering a 10× improvement in qubit error rates, 10× cost reduction per photonic quantum computer, and 4× faster quantum routing — without requiring any changes to existing Si, SiN, or thin-film LiNbO₃ fabrication platforms. Why it matters: Demonstrates that waveguide coupler geometry is a primary lever for scaling integrated photonic circuits — directly applicable to PIC design for both classical and quantum optical computing.

🔧 PIC Design & EDA

Keysight Completes VPIphotonics Acquisition — First End-to-End Photonic EDA Stack

Keysight completed its acquisition of VPIphotonics on June 9, 2026 (announced June 16), adding system-level link simulation (VPIphotonics Design Suite) to its existing RSoft (device-level FDTD/EME) and Photonic Designer (circuit-level) tools. The result is the industry's first integrated E-O-E simulation stack from waveguide physics through optical propagation to transceiver bit-error-rate — eliminating the need to transfer designs between separate tools. Why it matters: This EDA consolidation under one vendor mirrors what happened in electronic EDA in the 1990s; it will reshape vendor relationships, PDK requirements, and interoperability standards for PIC design teams globally over the next 2-3 years.

Photonic Inc. Raises C$180M — Largest Photonic Quantum Compute Fundraise of 2026
