NVIDIA Announces Spectrum-X Photonics Co-Packaged Optics Networking Switches

NVIDIA launched Spectrum-X Photonics CPO Ethernet and Quantum-X InfiniBand switches using silicon photonics integration, built on the Spectrum-6 ASIC platform delivering up to 409.6 Tb/s with 512 ports at 800 Gb/s. The design uses 4× fewer lasers and achieves 3.5× better power efficiency versus traditional pluggable optics; system vendors will offer Spectrum-X Photonics switches in 2H 2026. Why it matters: This is NVIDIA's direct move into CPO at scale — the laser reduction and efficiency gains set the bar that competing photonic interconnect approaches must now meet, and NVIDIA's buying power will accelerate supply-chain industrialization for SiPh CPO platforms across the AI factory market.

📄 Research & Labs

Novel Lidar System Maps Location, Speed, and Material Properties in a Single Measurement

University of Toronto and Ciena researchers adapted a coherent optical modem for lidar, enabling simultaneous millimeter-accurate ranging, velocity measurement, and surface material discrimination (metals, plastics, varying roughness) in a single scan using polarimetric full-wavefield coherent lidar. The system targets robotics, autonomous driving, and industrial inspection applications where multi-modal sensing is currently achieved by stacking separate sensors. Why it matters: Coherent lidar combining simultaneous Doppler and polarimetric readout in a compact form factor is directly relevant to PIC-integrated sensing heads — the technique aligns with on-chip coherent photonic architectures being developed for FMCW lidar.

HyperLight Raises $80M Series C to Accelerate TFLN Photonics for AI Infrastructure

Harvard spin-out HyperLight closed an $80M Series C led by MediaTek, with UMC Capital, Jabil, Foxconn, EDBI, and Qatar Investment Authority participating, to scale manufacturing of its thin-film lithium niobate (TFLN) Chiplet platform targeting 200G/lane and 400G/lane operations for AI data center interconnects. The round builds on a foundry partnership with UMC and Wavetek announced in March 2026. Why it matters: TFLN is the leading modulator technology for next-generation high-speed optical interconnects; HyperLight's UMC foundry partnership and blue-chip investor base signal that TFLN is crossing from research to production-ready supply chain — directly relevant to modulator technology selection for photonic compute interconnects.

Neurophos Raises $110M Series A for Metamaterial Optical Processing Unitscatch-up

Austin-based Neurophos raised $110M led by Gates Frontier (Bill Gates) with Microsoft M12, Aramco Ventures, and Bosch Ventures for photonic OPU chips using 10,000× miniaturized metamaterial optical modulators, claiming 100× efficiency over GPUs for AI inference, with first evaluation hardware expected in 2026 and commercial systems in 2028. This was the largest recent Series A in photonic compute at time of announcement. Why it matters: Neurophos represents a direct competitor in the optical AI accelerator space with a distinct modulator architecture (metamaterial vs. MZI/ring resonator) — understanding their performance and timeline claims is relevant to competitive positioning, and the Gates/Microsoft backing suggests serious enterprise channel access.
