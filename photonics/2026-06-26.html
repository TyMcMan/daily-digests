Physics-Informed Neural Networks Cut Photonic Component Design Training Data 10× — QNM-Net (OPN)catch-up

Swedish researchers combined quasi-normal mode (QNM) physics with neural networks to design photonic-crystal slabs and free-form metasurfaces, reducing training data requirements by 10× versus standard fully-connected networks. QNM-Net embeds energy conservation and causality constraints from Maxwell's equations directly into the NN architecture, improving both accuracy and generalization on component design tasks. Why it matters: for PIC design engineers using inverse-design or AI-driven layout tools, a physics-informed NN requiring 10× less training data directly reduces the electromagnetic simulation burden at the core of any ML-accelerated PDK component generator — and is relevant to Tidy3D/gdsfactory workflow integration.

📄 Research & Labs

Photonic Chips Advance Real-Time Learning in Spiking Neural Systems — All-Optical Activation (Optica)

Optica's news release covers a two-chip photonic neuromorphic system: a 16-channel photonic neuromorphic chip with 272 trainable parameters paired with a DFB laser array incorporating a saturable absorber that performs nonlinear activation entirely in the optical domain, eliminating the O-E conversion step. The system executed reinforcement learning tasks (CartPole balancing, Pendulum swing-up) with all computation remaining photonic. Why it matters: eliminating O-E-O conversion in the nonlinear activation function has been the stubborn design bottleneck in all-optical neural network accelerators; this demonstration using foundry-compatible components shows a concrete path to removing the last electronic bottleneck in optical inference pipelines.

Light-Driven Magnetic Memory Could Slash AI Data Center Energy Use — QST / Applied Physics Letterscatch-up

Japan's QST demonstrated magnetic memory switching via single ultrashort laser pulses — no electric current required — at ~1,000× faster switching speeds than conventional electrically-driven MRAM with lower heat generation. The underlying paper was published in Applied Physics Letters on June 8, 2026. Why it matters: laser-writable non-volatile memory is a potential enabler of O-E-O-free data center architectures, allowing photonic interconnects to write directly into memory without an electrical bridge; currently at materials demonstration stage but the 1,000× speed and photoelectric interface potential make this a priority item to track for future optical compute memory integration.

Deep Neural Network Inference on an Integrated Reconfigurable Photonic Tensor Processor — imec iSiPP50G (Nature Comms)catch-up

An imec-led team fabricated a silicon photonic tensor processor on the commercial iSiPP50G process (electro-absorption modulators + photodiodes), achieving 98.1% MNIST and 72.0% CIFAR-10 accuracy on a 9×3 optical crossbar, integrated into a standard 19-inch rack with a PyTorch software interface. Why it matters: iSiPP50G is a commercially accessible foundry node — this provides a reference design for photonic AI inference on silicon photonics that teams can build on or tape out from directly; the PyTorch interface lowers integration friction versus prior custom software stacks, making it the most deployable photonic inference reference to date.

🇪🇺 EU Photonics & Manufacturing

ASML Partners with TNO on 6-Inch InP Photonic Chip Pilot Line in Eindhoven

ASML formally joined TNO's new Photonic Chip Pilot Line at the High Tech Campus in Eindhoven, deploying DUV and i-line scanners for 6-inch indium phosphide wafer production under the EU PIXEurope project. Construction began in early 2026; first production capability is targeted for 2027. This marks the first time ASML has formally embedded its lithography equipment in a non-silicon (photonics) pilot manufacturing line. Why it matters: scaling InP to 6-inch halves per-die cost versus 4-inch at equal yield — the key economic hurdle for making InP-based photonic compute transceivers and modulators competitive with SiPho in high-volume production; EU-subsidized infrastructure under PIXEurope reduces single-company foundry risk for startups needing InP access.
