⚡ Photonic & Optical Compute

Quandela Demonstrates Photonic Quantum Reservoir Processing for ML and Single-Basis Quantum Tomography

A collaboration between Quandela, the Polish Academy of Sciences, and the University of Warsaw experimentally demonstrated a scalable Quantum Machine Learning architecture on a programmable silicon photonic QPU excited by single-photon states. The hardware-executed Quantum Reservoir Processing network performs both classical ML classification and quantum state tomography using a single fixed measurement basis — breaking the exponential scaling bottleneck for state characterization. Why it matters: Validates silicon-photonic QPU mesh architectures as production-viable ML accelerators tightly coupled to conventional HPC, providing a concrete workload demonstration relevant to heterogeneous photonic-electronic AI pipeline design.

Quandela Validates Low-Latency Photonic QPU Integration with NVIDIA NVQLink — GPU-QPU Latency 5,000 ms → 30 ms

Quandela demonstrated an FPGA-based controller bridge that cuts GPU-to-photonic-QPU round-trip latency from 5,000 ms to approximately 30 ms, using NVIDIA's NVQLink interface within standard HPC node architectures. This is the first validated low-latency integration between a photonic processor and NVIDIA's accelerated computing stack. Why it matters: Establishes a concrete I/O and latency budget for hybrid GPU+photonic QPU nodes — a key missing parameter for co-design of heterogeneous photonic compute fabrics targeting AI data center deployments.

🔧 PIC Design & EDA

Amorphous-Silicon Metasurface on SOI for Near-Vertical CPO Chip-to-Fiber Coupling (1310 nm)

A new paper in MDPI Photonics describes monolithically integrated amorphous-silicon metasurfaces on SOI at 1310 nm that emit near-vertically (0.88° angle) with a focal length of ~99 µm, replacing bulky external microlens arrays for chip-to-fiber or chip-to-detector coupling in co-packaged optics. The metasurface is fabricated monolithically on standard silicon photonics platforms. Why it matters: Eliminates the mechanical alignment tolerances currently constraining CPO packaging yield and cost — a potential drop-in on-chip optical interface component for PIC designers targeting CPO applications.

📄 Research & Labs

Modeling and Analysis of Phase Instability in Photonic Processors

Addresses the critical challenge of phase stability in reconfigurable multimode photonic processors used for quantum and classical optical information processing. Proposes two theoretical models — a Brownian random walk and an oscillating-harmonic phase reconstruction scheme — validated against experimental data on MZI-mesh photonic processors. Why it matters: Phase instability is the primary reliability gap limiting the scaling of photonic compute chips; these validated models provide a design-time framework for building stability guarantees into next-generation photonic processors.
