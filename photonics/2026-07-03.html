⚡ Photonic & Optical Compute

Q.ANT Runs Stable Diffusion and xLSTM on Native Photonic Processor at ISC 2026catch-up

At ISC High Performance 2026 in Hamburg, Q.ANT demonstrated a Stable Diffusion-style image model and an xLSTM recurrent network executing on its NPU Gen 2 — the first public demonstration of generative and sequential-forecasting workloads on a photonic processor, claiming up to 30× lower energy per matrix multiply than digital equivalents. The NPU 2 uses thin-film lithium niobate PICs where one optical element replaces 100–1,000 transistors for equivalent nonlinear functions. Why it matters: Moving beyond synthetic benchmarks to real generative-AI model execution is the credibility gate the optical-compute sector has needed; it validates that nonlinear photonics is ready for at least a subset of transformer-era workloads.

QuiX Quantum Unveils Dedalo: Fault-Tolerant QC via CMOS-Compatible SiN PICs

QuiX Quantum (Netherlands) released technical details of Dedalo, a fault-tolerant photonic quantum computing architecture that tackles the photon-loss problem by encoding logical qubits in a single measurement basis using CMOS-compatible silicon-nitride PICs compatible with room-temperature operation and Measurement-Based Quantum Computing. The modular design is intended to scale and interface with classical HPC clusters. Why it matters: If SiN PICs can host fault-tolerant logical qubits, the same foundry processes used today for optical interconnects and linear-optics accelerators become a direct substrate for hybrid classical-quantum optical compute nodes — a potential convergence point for photonic compute and photonic-QC supply chains.

Q/C Technologies (QCLS) Relocates HQ to SF, Opens Photonic Lab to Build OPU for AI Inference

Publicly traded Q/C Technologies moved its headquarters to San Francisco and is opening a 4,800 sq-ft integrated photonics lab to develop a proprietary Optical Processing Unit (OPU) for AI inference, targeting the energy and throughput bottlenecks of GPU-based inference. The company has hired engineers from IGP Photonics, Neurophos, and IonQ. Why it matters: A NASDAQ-listed company publicly pivoting to silicon-photonic OPU development signals growing investor confidence that optical inference acceleration is moving from research to commercial buildout — watch for procurement and partnership signals.

🔧 PIC Design & EDA

Wave Photonics PDK Platform Now Synopsys OptoCompiler Compatible — SiPh, SiN & InP

Wave Photonics announced that all PDKs distributed through its cloud-based PDK Management Platform — covering silicon photonics, SiN, and InP material platforms — are now validated for Synopsys OptoCompiler, the industry's first unified electronic-photonic design environment. CORNERSTONE's open-sourced PDKs are the first available to OptoCompiler users via the platform, with pre-calculated S-parameters enabling circuit simulation pre-tape-out. Why it matters: Reducing PDK setup friction for the dominant commercial EDA flow lowers the barrier for electronic-design teams to evaluate photonic process nodes — directly relevant for co-designing optical compute chiplets with CMOS.

📄 Research & Labs

Tyndall Achieves Two World-Firsts: Fully Optically Powered Chip-on-Tip Endoscope and FLIM Micro-Camera

Tyndall National Institute demonstrated (1) the world's first fully optically powered chip-on-tip micro-camera endoscope in a 3 mm catheter, and (2) the first chip-on-tip fluorescence lifetime imaging (FLIM) micro-camera at 4 mm diameter with a 128×120 SPAD array — both eliminating all electrical power cabling by routing power over optical fiber alongside signal. Why it matters: The underlying silicon-photonic power-over-fiber and PIC integration techniques are directly transferable to powering disaggregated optical compute elements inside dense server chassis without copper power rails.

Near-Perfect On-Chip Single-Photon Source via Ultrastrong Light-Matter Coupling (arXiv:2607.00574)

A new preprint proposes a deterministic single-photon source achieving near-unity emission efficiency by exploiting ultrastrong coupling between a two-level emitter and a cavity mode, dramatically suppressing multi-photon leakage compared to weak or strong-coupling regimes, analyzed for compatibility with on-chip photonic cavity architectures. Why it matters: High-purity on-chip single-photon sources are a key enabling component for photonic quantum computing and boson-sampling accelerators; improved efficiency directly reduces resource overhead of linear-optical quantum gates.

Hardware Fingerprinting for CPO Silicon Photonics via Photonic-Crystal Density Patterns (arXiv:2606.27612)

Biswas et al. (University of Florida) propose embedding 2D photonic-crystal arrays in the density-fill regions of a PIC — otherwise wasted space — to produce device-unique optical spectral fingerprints across visible/NIR wavelengths, verified by FDTD simulation in ANSYS Lumerical. Targets anti-counterfeiting and supply-chain trust for co-packaged-optics silicon photonics. Why it matters: As CPO transceivers enter AI rack-scale deployments at scale, hardware provenance becomes a system-security concern; this technique offers a non-invasive PIC-level identity layer with no extra area cost.
