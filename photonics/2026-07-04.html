📄 Research & Labs

Integrated Photon-Memory Entanglement via Dual Silicon-Carbide Resonators

A silicon-carbide photonic platform uses two microring resonators — one as an entangled photon-pair source, one as a cavity-enhanced atomic-frequency-comb quantum memory — to generate and verify photon-memory entanglement with 88.1 ± 10.6% single-pair visibility across up to 63 temporal modes. Why it matters: demonstrates a manufacturable telecom-band path to co-integrating quantum light sources and on-chip memories on the same PIC.

Brillouin Slow-Light in Nanowires Enables Lossless Single-Photon Storage

Hashem Zoubi proposes exploiting two counter-propagating pump fields inside a nanoscale waveguide to bring a single-photon signal to near-zero group velocity via stimulated Brillouin scattering, achieving photon storage without the gain or loss penalties that limit conventional buffer approaches. Why it matters: a passive, waveguide-compatible delay mechanism is a key missing component for on-chip quantum photonic circuits.

arxiv.org · 2026-07-02

Heriot-Watt LiDAR Method Measures Small Objects with Micron Accuracy

Derryck T. Reid's group at Heriot-Watt University demonstrated a fully-electronic LiDAR technique that maps aluminium objects with micron-scale precision from 40 cm, combining standard ranging with high-accuracy modes in a single acquisition — no complex optical setups required. Why it matters: opens a route to high-throughput, non-contact photonic metrology for precision parts inspection on factory lines.

Fusion Bionic (Dresden) Secures €8.2M to Scale DLIP Laser Texturing

Dresden-based Fusion Bionic closed an €8.2M package (€5.8M seed round from Stream Capital, TGFS, and MBG, plus €2.4M from industrial projects) to expand its Direct Laser Interference Patterning (DLIP) platform toward mass production, with an initial pilot line focused on anti-soiling laser texturing of solar glass. Why it matters: DLIP is a European-developed photonic surface-structuring technique with emerging industrial applications in solar, automotive, and semiconductor contexts.
