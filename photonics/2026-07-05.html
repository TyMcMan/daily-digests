📄 Research & Labs

Monolithic Integration of Piezo-Optomechanical Photonics and CMOS Electronics

Researchers fabricate 200 mm photonic wafers directly on completed CMOS driver wafers using back-end-of-line processing, connecting integrated piezoelectric actuators beneath silicon nitride waveguides to a high-density digital backplane with more than 2 million electrical connections per die at 6.4×6.4 µm electrode pitch. POMPICs combine low power consumption, high-speed modulation, cryogenic compatibility, and broadband optical transparency from UV to IR—all achieved without wafer bonding. Why it matters: first fully monolithic POMPI/CMOS co-fabrication, eliminating the alignment penalties and interface defects of hybrid bonding approaches.

Demonstrating Completeness in Optical Neural Computingcatch-up

Nature Communications paper presents a silicon photonic deep optical neural network integrating convolutional and fully connected layers with on-chip optoelectronic nonlinear activations, achieving 93.1% on MNIST, 90.9% on Fashion-MNIST, and 70.0% on CIFAR-10 entirely via photonic and optoelectronic operations. The work provides a formal proof that integrated optical neural networks are computationally complete—capable of expressing any function computable by a classical deep network. Why it matters: resolves a foundational open question and removes a key theoretical barrier to photonic AI accelerator adoption.
