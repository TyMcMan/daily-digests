📄 Research & Labs

Towards Topology-Aware Very Large-Scale Photonic AI Accelerators

Presents a modular scale-out architecture based on 4×4 photonic tensor core units and derives the "Symmetric Grid Rule": symmetric 2D grid topologies improve utilization by up to 6× while reducing memory access by over 40% compared to linear configurations, after accounting for insertion loss, fanout penalties, and laser power limits. The analysis establishes concrete bounds on single-chip scaling and motivates the modular multi-chip approach. Why it matters: provides the first systematic architectural guidance for scaling photonic matrix processors beyond single-chip implementations — the topology conclusions directly constrain floorplan choices for large photonic AI ASICs.

Harnessing Photonics for Machine Intelligence

Comprehensive survey mapping photonic device primitives — Mach-Zehnder interferometers, microring resonators, diffractive arrays, phase-change materials — to specific ML workload patterns, covering inference, training, and in-memory computing architectures. Identifies where each modality has a genuine energy or latency advantage over electronics and where the integration overhead erases the gain. Why it matters: rare synthesis of the photonics-ML design space in a single reference — useful for evaluating which photonic modalities are appropriate for which inference patterns in a photonic compute ASIC.
