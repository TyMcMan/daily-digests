📄 Research & Labs

University of Michigan Quantum Photonics: $4M NSF Phase 2 for Connectable Photonic Chips

A University of Michigan-led team received a $4M NSF Phase 2 award (building on a $1M pilot) to design field-deployable, connectable quantum photonic chips for precision measurements. If Phase 2 succeeds, Phase 3 targets a $55M NSF quantum center focused on prototyping quantum photonic chips at scale. Why it matters: The chip-to-chip connectivity and modularity challenges addressed here are architecturally analogous to those in classical photonic compute multi-chiplet systems — packaging and interface techniques developed here are worth tracking for CPO crossover.

Solid-State Sunlight-to-UV Photon Upconversion at 1.9% Efficiency (Optica OPN)

Japanese researchers demonstrated an organic semiconductor that upconverts 445 nm visible photons to UV via triplet–triplet annihilation at 1.9% efficiency under natural sunlight intensity alone — a first for ambient-light, solid-state operation. Primary targets are solar-driven photocatalysis and indoor UV sources. Why it matters: The triplet–triplet annihilation mechanism is of broader interest for on-chip wavelength conversion research and future UV photonic integration platforms, where efficient solid-state upconversion has been a longstanding challenge.

IQE Wins £1M+ Order for Advanced III-V Laser Epiwafers for Optical Interconnects

IQE, the Cardiff-based compound semiconductor epiwafer specialist, secured an order exceeding £1M to manufacture advanced III-V laser epitaxial structures for optical interconnect applications, with shipment targeted in H1 2027. Why it matters: IQE is one of the few volume-capable epiwafer suppliers for the laser chiplets that photonic interconnect startups depend on for heterogeneous PIC integration — this order signals sustained capital flowing into the InP/GaAs substrate supply chain that underpins CPO and NPO product development.

💰 Funding & Markets

Pilot Photonics Awarded €10.4M EIC Accelerator Investment for CPO Laser Scale-Up

Dublin-based Pilot Photonics received a recommended EIC Accelerator blended investment of up to €10.4M to scale its patented photonic chip technology — an integrated comb laser approach generating spectrally pure optical carriers critical for DWDM-based co-packaged optics (CPO) and 5G/6G fronthaul. The funding covers product qualification, high-volume manufacturing ramp, and international expansion. Why it matters: This is one of the largest recent EIC grants specifically targeting CPO photonic integration, putting Ireland's IPIC ecosystem on the CPO supply-chain map and signalling European investor confidence in integrated laser sources as a bottleneck worth funding.

AOP2026: International Conference on Applications of Optics and Photonics Opens in Lisbon (July 7–10)

The VII International Conference on Applications of Optics and Photonics (AOP2026) opened July 7 at ISEL Lisbon and runs through July 10, covering silicon photonics, nanophotonics, laser sources, sensing, and more. All accepted papers will be published open-access; extended versions are invited to the European Optical Society's JEOS-RP Topical Issue "Recent Advances on Optics and Photonics 2026." Why it matters: Watch for preprints appearing on arXiv in the coming weeks, particularly from integrated photonics and on-chip laser source sessions — AOP2026 is a leading venue for European PIC research and a useful signal for the EPIC/PhotonDelta ecosystem's research priorities.
