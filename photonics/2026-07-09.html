Q.ANT Runs Generative AI on Photonic Hardware at ISC 2026, Hamburgcatch-up

Q.ANT demonstrated a diffusion model and a recurrent neural network running entirely on their second-generation Native Processing Unit (NPU) at ISC 2026 in Hamburg — the first public demonstration of generative AI workloads executing fully on photonic hardware. The NPU uses integrated photonic matrix multiplication cores and operates at room temperature. Why it matters: Moving from inference on simpler tasks to generative models on photonic hardware is a significant capability milestone; the ISC venue signals readiness to engage the HPC/AI infrastructure buyer community.

Nature Photonics July 2026 — Vol. 20, Issue 7

The July 2026 issue of Nature Photonics includes: a bound-in-the-continuum (BIC) metasurface biosensor that detects tens of tumor-derived extracellular vesicles in 15 minutes; a technique for ultrafast spatio-temporal shaping of intense laser pulses enabling arbitrary 4D field control; and non-interferometric 3D reconstruction of dielectric tensors in complex materials. Why it matters: The BIC biosensing result is directly applicable to on-chip photonic diagnostics; the laser shaping work has implications for high-power photonic integration platforms.

General-Purpose Nonlinear Function Approximation via Linear Integrated Photonics

A preprint posted June 2026 demonstrates mathematically that a linear photonic integrated circuit — typically considered incapable of nonlinear computation — can implement arbitrary nonlinear functions when combined with appropriate input encoding and output measurement. The result provides a universal approximation theorem for linear PIC hardware and demonstrates it experimentally on a silicon photonics platform. Why it matters: This collapses the most-cited fundamental limitation of analog photonic compute; any PIC platform becomes a candidate for general nonlinear inference without adding active nonlinear elements.

Credo Completes $1.3B Acquisition of DustPhotonicscatch-up

Credo Technology Group closed its acquisition of DustPhotonics in May 2026 for approximately $1.3B in cash and stock. DustPhotonics is a silicon photonics PIC designer targeting AI datacenter interconnects; the combined entity now has an end-to-end vertically integrated stack: SerDes + DSP (Credo) + silicon photonics PIC (DustPhotonics) + system integration. Why it matters: Vertical integration of SerDes and SiPho is the key structural shift happening across the AI interconnect supply chain — this deal is a major example, directly competing with Marvell's and Broadcom's integrated CPO offerings.
