QuiX Quantum Shows Off a Photonic Architecture for HPC Datacenters (Dedalo)

QuiX Quantum published its Dedalo white paper outlining a fault-tolerant photonic quantum architecture combining integrated photonics and discrete-variable qubit encoding at room temperature, explicitly designed to co-locate with classical HPC and supercomputer infrastructure. The company argues that manufacturability, modularity, and energy efficiency must be solved simultaneously rather than sequentially — treating datacenter integration as the central design constraint rather than qubit fidelity alone. Why it matters: The datacenter-integration-first framing is directly relevant to hybrid classical-photonic system design; treating co-location with HPC racks as a hard constraint forces practical tradeoffs that are more useful to photonic compute architects than fidelity-maximizing approaches that ignore deployment context.

📄 Research & Labs

LightIN: Silicon-Integrated Photonic FPGA with AI Configuration Framework for Next-Generation AI Clusters

Published in Light: Science & Applications (vol. 15, article 165), LightIN is a programmable silicon photonic chip integrating 40 reconfigurable unit cells with over 160 components, supporting on-chip computing, signal switching, and encryption from a single platform with an intelligent software configuration framework. The field-programmable photonic concept — analogous to an electrical FPGA but realised in silicon photonics — is designed to accelerate design-to-tape-out cycles for reconfigurable photonic compute fabrics via standardised, software-driven circuit configuration. Why it matters: Software-configurable photonic hardware is the step between fixed-function PICs and truly programmable optical compute; LightIN's 40-cell integration density and AI-assisted configuration framework are a concrete demonstration of this programmability at research scale.

Integrated Photonic 3D Tensor Processing Engine Running at 10–30 GHz

Also in Light: Science & Applications (vol. 15, article 154), this work demonstrates a 3D tensor processing engine (3D-TPE) based on interleaved time, wavelength, and spatial-domain modulation, experimentally running at 10–30 GHz clock speed and achieving 97.06% accuracy on LiDAR point-cloud classification. Data caching, channel synchronisation, and computation are all realised in the optical domain, substantially reducing DRAM round-trip bottlenecks compared to electronic tensor processors. Why it matters: A full optical datapath for tensor operations — rather than just a matrix-multiply kernel — is a significant architectural step toward photonic AI accelerators that do not just offload one operator but can sustain a complete inference pipeline in the optical domain.

💰 Funding & Markets

Defiance Launches Europe's First Photonics UCITS ETF (PHOT) on LSE and Borsa Italiana

Defiance ETFs launched the PHOT UCITS ETF on the London Stock Exchange and Borsa Italiana — Europe's first fund dedicated exclusively to photonic technologies including PICs, optical interposers, transceivers, modulators, and lasers. At 0.69% TER within a $162M UCITS AUM platform, PHOT provides mainstream institutional investors a direct route into the photonic compute and interconnect supply chain without picking individual stocks. Why it matters: An index ETF dedicated to photonics signals mainstream institutional conviction that the sector is entering a durable growth phase; it expands the investor base for photonics suppliers and validates commercial roadmap timelines the industry has been pitching to early-stage venture.

Aylight Closes €4.5M Pre-Seed to Commercialize Chip-Scale FM Comb Laser for AI WDM Interconnects

ETH Zürich spin-out Aylight raised €4.5M co-led by Elaia and Swisscom Ventures to commercialize a chip-scale frequency-modulated comb (FM comb) laser that generates many precisely spaced wavelengths from a single photonic chip, targeting AI datacenter WDM optical interconnects and FMCW sensing. The raise is an explicit response to the laser supply crunch that drove Nvidia's $4B commitment to Coherent and Lumentum in March 2026 — Aylight's approach would make WDM sources chip-integrable rather than module-level discrete assemblies. Why it matters: A compact on-chip multiwavelength source addresses one of the hardest integration challenges in WDM photonic compute interconnects; if manufacturable at volume, it removes a discrete component bottleneck that currently limits scaling of wavelength-division-multiplexed photonic inference fabrics.

CCRAFT Raises $7.8M to Scale World's First Independent TFLN Photonic Chip Foundry

CCRAFT (spun out of CSEM in April 2025) closed a heavily oversubscribed $7.8M seed round led by QBIT Capital to scale its independent thin-film lithium niobate (TFLN) photonic chip foundry in Neuchâtel, Switzerland, targeting 2,000 wafers/month by 2030. TFLN platforms deliver electro-optic bandwidths and half-wave voltages well beyond silicon photonics, making them critical for 800G+ and beyond optical interconnects in AI data infrastructure. Why it matters: As the only independent pure-play TFLN foundry globally, CCRAFT's capacity expansion directly de-risks the TFLN supply chain for photonic compute and quantum hardware teams that cannot access adequate volume through vertically integrated suppliers — a foundry-access gap that constrains the entire TFLN-based interconnect roadmap.

Xanadu Opens US Hub in Albany, NY — Embedding PIC Design Teams in Semiconductor Research Corridor

Photonic quantum computing company Xanadu opened a US operations hub in Albany, NY, co-locating hardware design teams with Corning, Applied Materials, DISCO, and EV Group to optimise mass production of on-chip GKP qubits using standard semiconductor lithography lines. The US workforce has grown more than 5× since 2023. Why it matters: Xanadu's playbook — co-locating photonic chip design with established semiconductor foundry partners rather than building captive fabs — is a viable template for scaling photonic chip production beyond prototyping, and the partner list (Corning, Applied Materials) signals which supply-chain relationships matter for PIC volume manufacturing.

New Optical Technique Captures Refractive Index, Absorption, and Film Thickness in One Non-Destructive Pass

Researchers demonstrated a new optical measurement method capable of extracting refractive index, absorption coefficient, and film thickness simultaneously in a single non-destructive measurement, reducing metrology time in photonic device development cycles. Integrated photonic chips require precise simultaneous control of these parameters across waveguide layers; multi-property optical metrology removes the need for sequential single-property tools. Why it matters: For SiN and TFLN platform development — where layer stack characterisation is a recurring bottleneck in design-to-fab iteration — this technique directly accelerates process qualification and inline yield monitoring by collapsing three separate metrology steps into one.

Pasqal / Aeponyx Launch PIC Packaging Center of Competency at C2MI, Targeting 500K Modules/Year

Pasqal subsidiary Aeponyx partnered with HOP Technologies, Phantom Photonics, and C2MI to launch a PIC Packaging Center of Competency in Bromont, Quebec, backed by $7.9M in government funding, with a roadmap to standardize PIC assembly and packaging processes and scale to 500,000+ PIC modules per year in a later phase using purpose-built Aixemtec packaging equipment. Why it matters: Packaging remains the principal bottleneck between chip-level PIC yield and system-level deployment — this centre attacks that gap by building standardised, high-volume assembly pipelines for quantum-grade photonic chips; standardised PIC packaging processes developed here are transferable to any photonic compute hardware effort bridging chips to fiber-optic or free-space systems at volume.
