The Aspirations of HPE and Dell in the Quantum-Classical HPC Datacenter

At HPE Discover 2026, HPE announced partnerships with IQM Quantum Computers, Quantinuum, QuEra, and Rigetti to create hybrid quantum-classical testbeds integrated with Cray supercomputers; Dell is pursuing parallel strategies with different vendor alliances. ORCA Photonic TN-2 + Hybrid AI Yields Measurable Drug Discovery Gain

A TU Denmark study combined ORCA Computing's photonic TN-2 processor with Sparrow Quantum and a classical generative AI model to screen immune-targeting peptides, yielding roughly 6.3 additional high-probability drug-binding sequences per 1,000 generated compared to classical-only runs — published in a Nature partner journal. This is the first peer-reviewed benchmark of a commercially available photonic processor delivering a quantified AI workflow advantage in a real-world biomedical task. Why it matters: Validates the hybrid classical-photonic pipeline model at publication quality, setting a reproducible baseline that compresses the credibility gap for photonic AI accelerator procurement conversations.

AI-Driven Thermal Mapping and Management in 3D Integrated Photonic Circuits

University of Florida, ficonTEC, and Colorado State researchers present a dual-method framework combining an AI thermal-modeling layer with a design-based heuristic to predict and correct hot spots across stacked layers in 3D photonic integrated circuits, validated against real PIC packaging geometries used in high-performance computing. Thermal crosstalk between stacked optical layers is a leading scaling bottleneck for photonic AI accelerators. Why it matters: Offers a production-validated path to managing the thermal penalty of 3D PIC integration — directly applicable to die-stacked photonic compute architectures targeting HPC and data center deployments.

Design and Benchmarking of a Quantum Photonic Chip (RP000)

Rotonium, NUS Centre for Quantum Technologies, Inveriant, Politecnico di Milano, and CNIT introduce RP000, a room-temperature CMOS-compatible photonic quantum processor encoding quantum states in degrees of freedom of single photons using standard silicon photonics foundry processes; on ML classification benchmarks it outperforms classical networks of comparable size with superior noise resilience versus superconducting QPUs. Why it matters: Establishes a commercially manufacturable quantum photonic co-processor baseline using existing SiPh foundry infrastructure — the CMOS compatibility removes the clean-room barrier that has limited photonic quantum compute to specialist fabs.

Imec and Diraq Demonstrate 8-Qubit Silicon Spin Array on CMOS-Compatible Platform

Imec and Diraq demonstrated coherent operation and readout of an 8-qubit silicon MOS spin-qubit array on imec's 300 mm CMOS-compatible platform (the same iSiPP process line used for silicon photonics), with results published in Nature Communications. The result validates imec's shared-foundry model for both SiPh and qubit arrays on the same process infrastructure. Why it matters: Photonic and qubit co-integration is approaching manufacturing readiness at a top-tier foundry — direct implication for co-packaged photonic-qubit devices and the European PIC supply chain.

Q-PLANET Launches €50M EU Pilot Line for Neutral Atom Quantum Chips

Q-PLANET, a €50 million EU Horizon Europe program led by Pasqal across 28 organizations in 11 EU Member States, has launched to build industrial-grade neutral atom quantum chip supply chains including photonic trapping optics, waveguide-coupled atom interfaces, and high-efficiency single-photon detectors. The component overlap with PIC supply chains — optical waveguides, fiber array units, single-photon sources — is substantial and creates cross-program demand for EU PIC capacity. Why it matters: Europe's push for a vertically integrated photonic and quantum chip supply chain is gaining program-level funding at scale, reinforcing the EU as a strategic geography for foundry capacity investment.
