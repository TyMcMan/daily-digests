Atomica Launches AI Optical Source Platform to Address Laser Bottleneck in AI Infrastructurecatch-up

Atomica launched a microfabrication platform targeting compact laser sources, optical engines, fiber coupling systems, and thermally stable optical interconnects for AI infrastructure. The company operates out of a 30,000 sq ft Class 100 cleanroom near Santa Barbara and positions itself as a foundry partner for the gap between chip-level photonics and deployable optical I/O modules. Why it matters: laser sourcing, stabilization, and coupling remain the most under-addressed integration layers in co-packaged optics; a dedicated fab platform targeting this layer directly de-risks supply for CPO and SiPho integrations at Akhetonics scale.

kfactory v3.0.0 — Major Version with Breaking API Changes

kfactory (the KLayout-backend variant of gdsfactory) released v3.0.0 with breaking changes: the port attribute renamed from any_cross_section to cross_section, changed default port-connection behavior for mirrored ports, and spatial overlap pruning cleanup. Why it matters: any existing kfactory-based PIC layout scripts will require migration before running against updated gdsfactory versions — pin the kfactory version in CI immediately and schedule migration to avoid silent geometry errors.

cspdk Adds ThermalPhaseShifter Active Model for si220 C-Bandcatch-up

The Cornerstone Silicon Photonics PDK added a ThermalPhaseShifter circulax active compact model for the si220 C-band stack, extending the PDK's active device library beyond passive routing and enabling thermally tunable MZI and switch designs within the gdsfactory ecosystem. Why it matters: thermo-optic modulators are a critical active component for optical compute fabrics; native cspdk support removes a manual model-import step for Cornerstone tape-out designs.

MIT and Samsung Extend QD-LED Lifespan by Up to 5,000×

MIT and Samsung demonstrated that encapsulating QD-LEDs in an acrylate-based resin increases red LED lifespan 8× and blue LED lifespan more than 5,000×, removing the primary commercialization barrier for quantum dot emitter devices (published Science Advances, July 15). Why it matters: blue QD emitter stability was previously 50–100× worse than red — the leading reliability blocker for QD-based photonic sources used in integrated photonic platforms and optical interconnects; this result essentially resolves it using standard encapsulation materials.
