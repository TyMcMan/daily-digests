⚡ Photonic & Optical Compute

Chinese Researchers Build Optical AI Inference Network Claiming 100× Speedupcatch-up

A Peking University team interconnected GPU-class compute chips via a 16×16 all-optical switch chip paired with 400 Gb/s silicon photonic transceivers, achieving 6.4 Tb/s total switching bandwidth. The cluster demonstrated >100× inference speedup versus a commercial A100 configuration at approximately one-ninth the power draw. Published in National Science Review; covered by IEEE Spectrum, SCMP, and eWeek. Why it matters: Validates the chip-to-chip all-optical switch fabric as a practical path for AI inference clusters — the 400 Gb/s SiPh transceiver integration and switch architecture are directly relevant design reference points for optical-compute interconnect stacks.

QuiX Quantum Announces Carina — First Universal Photonic QC for Data Center Deploymentcatch-up

QuiX Quantum delivered Carina to Germany’s DLR Quantum Computing Initiative: a room-temperature, rack-mountable universal photonic quantum computer using single photons as qubits at telecom wavelengths. The system supports 8 input / 4 computational qubits in a universal gate-based architecture with no cryogenics required, designed to co-exist with existing HPC/AI rack infrastructure. Why it matters: First universal gate-based photonic QC formally qualified for data center deployment; telecom-wavelength operation means standard fiber infrastructure is directly reusable — a significant milestone for hybrid classical/quantum photonic system integration and near-term co-packaged photonic compute architectures.

📄 Research & Labs

Fully Programmable Slow-Light Photonic Integrated Circuit on 0.25 mm² Chip (SNU / University of Seoul)catch-up

Joint Seoul National University and University of Seoul team demonstrated a 1D coupled-resonator-induced transparency (CRIT) lattice PIC enabling fully programmable slow light with tunable delay lines, reconfigurable synchronization, and linear frequency conversion — all within 0.25 mm² at telecom wavelengths. The architecture is software-configurable in real time with no physical reconfiguration. Published in Advanced Science (Wiley); covered by PIC Magazine and phys.org. Why it matters: Software-defined optical timing and buffering on a single telecom-wavelength chip is a critical capability for chip-to-chip optical interconnect architectures where signal synchronization and delay control are key design constraints in optical-compute fabrics.

Room-Temperature Material That Filters Light by Its Quantum Statisticscatch-up

Researchers engineered a material class that selectively transmits or suppresses quantum light states by distinguishing “allowed” and “forbidden” photon-statistical bands, operating without cryogenics. The material acts as a passive filter on the quantum statistics of incoming light rather than on wavelength or polarization. Why it matters: Points toward room-temperature quantum-statistics-selective optical components — a potential building block for quantum-classical hybrid photonic processors without the cryogenic overhead that currently limits quantum photonic compute to research labs.
