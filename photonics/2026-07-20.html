China's DISH Method Cuts 3D Optical Chip Production from Hours to Seconds

A Chinese research team has developed DISH (Digital Incoherent Synthesis of Holographic light fields), a holographic patterning technique that produces complex multi-layer 3D optical chip structures in seconds rather than the hours required by conventional layer-by-layer methods. The method synthesises multi-layer photonic structures simultaneously using a spatial light modulator-based holographic field, eliminating sequential lithography steps. The team claims DISH is compatible with existing photonics foundry infrastructure. Why it matters: If manufacturable at foundry scale, volumetric 3D PIC integration would unlock interconnect density far beyond planar SiPh limits — directly relevant to optical compute architectures that need extreme I/O density.

Vector Photonics Unveils AMPPS Packaging to Accelerate PCSEL Adoption in AI Data Centers

Vector Photonics announced AMPPS (Advanced Modular Photonic Packaging Solution), a packaging technology designed to bring photonic crystal surface-emitting lasers (PCSELs) to the AI data center market at scale. PCSELs deliver superior beam quality, efficiency, and brightness compared to VCSELs — attractive for high-density optical interconnects — but have been limited by costly custom packaging. AMPPS provides a modular, manufacturable assembly path enabling standard high-volume processes to be applied to PCSEL-based optical engines for intra-rack and free-space links. Why it matters: A manufacturable PCSEL packaging route could displace VCSELs in short-reach interconnects within AI clusters, improving energy efficiency at the light-source level for integrated photonic compute architectures.

Reality-Infused Deep Learning Framework May Revolutionize Nanophotonic Inverse Design

Researchers introduced a "reality-infused" deep learning framework for nanophotonic inverse design that embeds experimentally measured fabrication variability directly into the neural network training loop, closing the gap between simulation-predicted and as-fabricated device performance. Conventional simulation-driven inverse design often fails in practice because real devices deviate from idealized models due to etch depth variation, roughness, and material non-uniformity; this approach co-trains on both simulated and measured device data. Why it matters: Inverse design tools that predict manufacturable geometries rather than simulation-optimal ones would be transformative for Akhetonics' PIC design flow — reducing iteration cycles between design and tapeout verification.

Inverse-Designed TFLN Photonic Interfaces Exceed Eigenmode Coupling Limits

Researchers report inverse-designed edge-coupling interfaces on the thin-film lithium niobate (TFLN) platform achieving simulated/measured fiber-to-chip insertion losses as low as 1 dB / 3 dB per facet with 1-dB bandwidths exceeding 120 nm — performance unattainable by conventional eigenmode taper designs on TFLN due to its partially etched rib waveguide geometry and non-vertical sidewalls. The couplers require only a single high-resolution lithography step, making them directly insertable into standard TFLN PDK design flows for foundry tapeout. Why it matters: Efficient fiber-chip coupling is a persistent yield and insertion-loss bottleneck for TFLN EO modulator and frequency-converter chips; this result demonstrates a practical, foundry-compatible inverse-design path for one of the hardest TFLN packaging challenges.

Laser-Based Digital Security System Achieves Near-Zero False Positive Rates

Researchers demonstrated a photonic physically-unclonable-function (PUF) device that generates cryptographic keys from the unique laser "fingerprint" of each photonic chip — no two chips produce the same speckle pattern. Combining photonics with AI classification delivers near-zero false-positive authentication rates and real-time throughput. Why it matters: As photonic compute chips proliferate in AI infrastructure, on-chip hardware root-of-trust based on optical PUFs becomes directly relevant to supply-chain integrity and hardware attestation — a security surface Akhetonics will face as it scales to production.

Integrated Lithium Niobate Electro-Optic Frequency Comb Enables High-Precision FMCW 3D Ranging

A Nature Photonics paper demonstrates an integrated LiNbO3 electro-optic frequency comb that simultaneously tracks frequency variation in an FMCW laser and provides the broadband coherent comb teeth needed for high-precision 3D scene ranging. The chip-scale EO comb generates hundreds of phase-locked sidebands from a single CW input with low drive voltage, enabling accurate laser chirp calibration across the full sweep bandwidth. Why it matters: Chip-scale EO combs on LiNbO3 have direct application to optical computing clock distribution and coherent sensing integration — the FMCW LiDAR demonstration validates the EO comb platform's precision for timing-critical photonic systems.
