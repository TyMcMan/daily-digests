NVIDIA Reveals Plan to Scale AI Factories with Co-Packaged Optics

NVIDIA's Spectrum-X Photonics CPO Ethernet switches will deliver up to 409.6 Tb/s in H2 2026, and the Quantum-X InfiniBand switches move to CPO in the Feynman GPU generation (2028). Both scale-out and scale-up networking paths now have CPO on the roadmap, marking NVIDIA's formal commitment to optical interconnects as the primary medium for next-generation AI infrastructure. Why it matters: When NVIDIA commits to CPO across both IB and Ethernet product lines simultaneously, it closes the "will CPO actually ship?" debate — suppliers, packaging foundries, and AI system designers can now plan around optical interconnect as a given for high-end AI clusters from 2026 onward.

UMC Delivers First Mass-Produced 1.6T Silicon Photonics Wafers; IMEC iSiPP300 to Open Broader Access in 2027

UMC's Singapore 12-inch fab has delivered mass-produced silicon photonics wafers to SILITH Technology at 1.6T data rates — establishing a non-TSMC, high-volume SiPh manufacturing path aimed squarely at AI data center interconnects. Separately, IMEC's iSiPP300 silicon photonics process is set to open its platform to additional customers from 2027, widening the foundry ecosystem for volume SiPh production. Why it matters: TSMC has dominated advanced SiPh capacity; a UMC 12-inch SiPh path at 1.6T gives fabless SiPh designers a strategic second source and lowers supply-chain concentration risk at exactly the moment hyperscaler demand is surging.

IEEE Study: Micro-Transfer Printing Advances Silicon Photonics Heterogeneous Integration

Researchers from Ghent University and imec published in IEEE's Journal of Lightwave Technology showing that micro-transfer printing (MTP) can integrate III-V semiconductors and lithium niobate directly onto CMOS-compatible silicon photonics platforms. MTP enables co-integration of on-chip gain materials (needed for integrated lasers) and high-speed modulators without exotic bonding processes that have historically limited heterogeneous SiPh yields. Why it matters: On-chip laser integration and high-speed LiNbO3 modulators are two of SiPh's biggest material integration gaps; MTP as a CMOS-compatible route resolves both simultaneously and could unlock fully integrated photonic compute chiplets without hybrid bonding.

Inverse-Designed Meta Processing Units for Multi-Task Near-Field Photonic Computingcatch-up

Introduces compact passive matrix operators (9.6 × 4.8 μm footprint) called meta processing units (MPUs), implemented as inverse-designed shallow-etched silicon regions acting as local complex matrix transformations. Demonstrates 92.7% fidelity on 4×4 matrix operations and 83.5%/80.9% dual-task vowel recognition accuracy when paired with active MZI neurons. Why it matters: Compact, general, task-reconfigurable matrix operators have been a missing piece for scaling photonic neural networks — MPUs solve footprint, generality, and reconfigurability simultaneously in a fabrication-compatible form.

FieldCore: Fully Multiplexed Photonic Tensor Computing Across All Five Optical Dimensionscatch-up

FieldCore simultaneously exploits all five native optical multiplexing dimensions — wavelength, radio-frequency, guided-mode, time, and space — in a single inverse-designed silicon photonic chip, enabling parallelism to scale multiplicatively rather than additively. Experimentally validated on ultra-high-baudrate arithmetic, image convolution, and parallel handwritten-digit recognition. Why it matters: The most complete demonstration of full-field photonic tensor parallelism to date; shows a concrete path to scaling silicon photonic AI accelerators beyond the bandwidth ceilings imposed by single-dimensional multiplexing.

Why it matters: Sets a new experimental throughput benchmark for silicon photonic AI accelerators and validates the TSWDM architecture as a credible path to multi-TOPS photonic inference — the first time all three multiplexing dimensions have been combined experimentally at this bandwidth.
