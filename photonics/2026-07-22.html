📄 Research & Labs

Interactively Addressable Organic Metadevicespaper

Researchers at the University of Stuttgart demonstrate organic thin-film metadevices in which individual pixels can be addressed and switched in real time, enabling spatial light modulation at the device level. The approach merges organic semiconductor processing with metasurface design, yielding dynamic wavefront control without mechanical parts. Why it matters: Electrically reconfigurable metasurfaces are a key enabling technology for beam-steering, holography, and integrated photonic signal processing; an organic-layer route could lower fabrication costs relative to III-V or MEMS approaches.

QinetiQ Opens Helena High-Energy Laser to Foreign Partners at Farnborough 2026defence

QinetiQ used the Farnborough International Airshow to publicly offer the £20 M Helena laser weapon system — already under UK MoD contract — to allied nations. Helena delivers multi-kilowatt continuous-wave output for counter-drone and force-protection roles and has completed live-fire trials. Why it matters: High-energy laser weapons represent one of the highest-volume growth markets for beam-combining fibre and solid-state laser modules, creating near-term demand for EU and UK photonics supply chains.
