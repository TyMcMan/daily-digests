🔧 PIC Design & EDA

gdsfactory v9.46.0 — kfactory v3 migration, route_bundle consolidation, grid fix

gdsfactory v9.46.0 (July 22, 2026, 05:08 UTC) completes the migration to kfactory v3, replacing all legacy routing strategies with a unified route_bundle API. Key bug fixes: negative bend_s_offset values now work correctly; grid_factor being silently ignored in snap_to_grid is resolved; select_ports performance is improved via precomputed layer filters; via_stack_with_offset bugs are fixed. Documentation has migrated from Jupyter Book to Zensical. Why it matters: The kfactory v3 dependency update requires workflow migration testing for any existing PIC design scripts; the grid-snapping and via_stack fixes directly affect layout accuracy and tapeout verification in high-density designs.

🇪🇺 EU Photonics & Manufacturing

Photonics21 welcomes EU Chips Act 2.0 inclusion of photonics, calls for dedicated European Photonics Partnershipcatch-up

Photonics21 formally welcomed photonics' inclusion in the EU Chips Act 2.0 framework and called for establishment of a dedicated European Photonics Partnership — modeled on the existing Horizon Europe Photonics Partnership — with a Board of Stakeholders election targeted for fall 2026. The move positions photonics alongside advanced silicon logic and compound semiconductors as a strategic EU technology. Why it matters: For Akhetonics, this signals continued and potentially expanded EU public funding instruments (KDT JU calls, EIC Accelerator, strategic procurement) and stronger ecosystem support for integrated photonic hardware across EU foundries.

💰 Funding & Markets

PsiQuantum signs $125M DARPA Stage C agreement for photonic quantum compute validation

PsiQuantum secured a $125M expanded DARPA agreement under the Quantum Benchmarking Initiative (Stage C — the final, most advanced phase), covering photonic assembly, cryogenic validation, advanced material manufacturing, and algorithmic resource benchmarking for its fault-tolerant photonic quantum computer fabricated in CMOS fabs (GlobalFoundries). DARPA conducted on-site technical reviews and component-level testing throughout 2025. Why it matters: Government validation of silicon-photonics manufacturing processes meeting quantum-grade tolerances is a strong signal for the precision and yield requirements of PIC-based compute hardware at volume — the same foundry stack is relevant to integrated photonic compute more broadly.

Photon Queue raises $4M oversubscribed seed for room-temperature photon memory

Photon Queue (UIUC spinout, founded 2024) closed an oversubscribed $4M seed led by Playground Global to commercialize room-temperature quantum memory that stores and synchronizes single photons using free-space linear optics — no cryogenics, vacuum, or quantum transduction required. Early contracts with Sandia National Laboratories and University of Maryland validate demand across photonic, trapped-ion, and neutral-atom quantum platforms. Why it matters: Photon buffering and synchronization are unsolved but critical challenges in feed-forward optical compute architectures; this company's room-temperature linear-optics approach is the closest to PIC-compatible implementation with proven customer traction.
