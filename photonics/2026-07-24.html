Northwestern Researchers Demonstrate Quantum Entanglement Over Busy Telecom Fiber

Northwestern University researchers have demonstrated quantum entanglement distribution over a standard, actively used metropolitan-area fiber network — coexisting with classical traffic without isolation. The experiment used wavelength multiplexing to share fiber with conventional data channels, showing that quantum networking infrastructure does not require dedicated dark fiber.

Why it matters: Eliminating the dark-fiber requirement dramatically reduces deployment cost and accelerates the timeline for practical metropolitan quantum networks built on existing telecom infrastructure.

catch-up Quantum Source: Calibration-Free Single-Atom Entangled Photon Source for Quantum Networks

Quantum Source has published results for a single-atom-based entangled photon source that operates without manual calibration. The system generates on-demand high-fidelity entangled photon pairs with sub-nanosecond timing jitter, intended for integration into fiber-based quantum repeater chains and quantum network nodes.

Why it matters: Calibration-free photon sources are a prerequisite for operationally practical quantum networks — removing the continuous manual alignment burden that has limited deployment of earlier lab systems.

🔧 PIC Design & EDA

catch-up Photon Design Integrates HAROLD QD with PICWave for 3D Quantum-Dot Laser Simulation

Photon Design has integrated its HAROLD quantum-dot (QD) gain modeling tool with PICWave, enabling full 3D simulation of QD lasers including carrier dynamics, optical mode profiles, and thermal effects within a single PIC simulation environment. The integration covers both FP and DFB laser geometries.

Why it matters: Quantum-dot lasers are increasingly used in co-packaged optics and silicon photonics platforms; accurate 3D simulation reduces tape-out risk and shortens the design iteration cycle for QD-based PICs.

🇪🇺 EU Photonics & Manufacturing

Photonic Chips as the Edge of Dutch Defense Systems

A study by PhotonDelta and the Netherlands Ministry of Defence maps the role of integrated photonic chips across Dutch defense applications: LIDAR-based sensing, optical signal processing for radar, secure quantum key distribution links, and miniaturized optical gyroscopes for inertial navigation. The report recommends domestic photonics manufacturing capacity as a strategic sovereignty priority.

Why it matters: Defense demand is emerging as a distinct demand driver for EU integrated photonics — separate from the datacom/AI compute narrative that has dominated recent ecosystem discourse, and one with longer procurement cycles and stricter supply-chain requirements.

Photon Queue Raises Oversubscribed $4M Seed Round to Scale Quantum Memory Systems

Photon Queue has closed a $4M seed round — described as oversubscribed — to scale production of its quantum memory hardware. The company's system stores and retrieves single photons on demand, a critical component for quantum repeaters and long-distance quantum networking. Investors include quantum-focused hardware VCs.

Why it matters: Quantum memory is the key bottleneck for extending quantum networks beyond point-to-point links; seed-stage funding at this scale indicates investor confidence that hardware-level solutions are approaching commercial readiness.

catch-up Luna Innovations to Be Acquired by TJC Private Equity Group

Luna Innovations, the US-based photonics test and measurement company, has agreed to be acquired by TJC, a private equity firm. Luna's product lines include optical frequency domain reflectometry (OFDR) instruments and distributed sensing solutions for aerospace, defense, and industrial applications. The deal is expected to close in Q3 2026.

Why it matters: Luna's OFDR technology underpins precision fiber characterization widely used in PIC fabrication and qualification; a PE acquisition signals either consolidation in the photonics T&M market or a repositioning of the asset toward a strategic buyer.
